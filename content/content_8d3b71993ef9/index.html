This table details alleged UFO sightings in the United States, spanning from 1865 to 2023. Each entry includes the date, city, state, a link to a main article if available, and a brief description of the event.

  • September 1865, Cadotte Pass, Montana: Trapper James Lumley reported witnessing a craft crash, describing sulfur smells and hieroglyphics.
  • 1896â€“1897, Multiple Locations, Multiple States: Sightings of airship-like vehicles were reported across the United States during the Mystery Airship wave.
  • April 17, 1897, Aurora, Texas: Residents claimed an airship crashed, and the alien pilot was buried in the local cemetery.
  • February 24, 1942, Los Angeles, California: The “Battle of Los Angeles” involved an alleged enemy attack and anti-aircraft artillery barrage. Secretary of the Navy Frank Knox called it a “false alarm,” later attributed to “war nerves” and a weather balloon.
  • August 1945, Jornada del Muerto, New Mexico: Two boys reported seeing an “avocado-shaped” object crash, containing insect-like alien creatures, shortly after the first atomic bomb detonation.
  • June 21, 1947, Maury Island, Washington: An incident reported before the Kenneth Arnold sighting, involving an alleged encounter with “men in black.”
  • June 24, 1947, Mount Rainier, Washington: Kenneth Arnold reported seeing nine unusual objects flying in formation near Mount Rainier, describing them as bright, erratic, and fast.
  • July 1947, Roswell, New Mexico: The Roswell UFO Incident involved the recovery of materials near Roswell, sparking speculation about a crashed alien craft versus a top-secret research balloon.
  • January 7, 1948, Maysville, Kentucky: The Mantell Incident involved the death of Captain Thomas F. Mantell, a Kentucky Air National Guard pilot, while pursuing a UFO.
  • July 24, 1948, Montgomery, Alabama: The Chiles-Whitted UFO Encounter involved two commercial pilots reporting a near-collision with a torpedo-shaped object.
  • October 1, 1948, Fargo, North Dakota: The Gorman Dogfight involved a US Air Force pilot sighting and pursuing a UFO for 27 minutes.
  • May 11, 1950, McMinnville, Oregon: Paul and Evelyn Trent took the McMinnville UFO photographs on their farm.
  • May 29, 1950, Mount Vernon, Maryland: Captain Willis Sperry and crewmembers on an American Airlines DC-6 reported a “brilliant, diffused, bluish light.”
  • August 15, 1950, Great Falls, Montana: The Mariana UFO incident garnered national media attention during a period of high interest in UFOs and alien invasion.
  • August 25, 1951, Lubbock, Texas: The Lubbock Lights were a v-shaped formation of lights seen over the town.
  • May 24, 1952, Burbank, California: Orfeo Angelucci claimed to encounter flying saucers and friendly human-appearing pilots during his drives home from an aircraft plant.
  • July 13, 1952, Washington, D.C.: The 1952 Washington, D.C. UFO incident involved a series of unidentified flying object reports over the city.
  • September 12, 1952, Flatwoods, West Virginia: Six boys and a woman reported seeing a UFO landing and a bizarre-looking creature.
  • November 23, 1953, Sault Ste. Marie, Michigan: Felix Moncla, Jr., a US Air Force pilot, disappeared with 2nd Lt. Robert Wilson while pursuing a UFO over Lake Superior.
  • November 2, 1957, Levelland, Texas: The Levelland UFO case involved multiple witnesses reporting unusual aerial phenomena.
  • September 19, 1961, Lancaster, New Hampshire: Betty and Barney Hill claimed to have been abducted by extraterrestrials.
  • April 24, 1964, Socorro, New Mexico: Lonnie Zamora, a New Mexico police officer, reported a UFO close encounter.
  • September 3, 1965, Exeter, New Hampshire: The Exeter incident involved a large UFO sighting by a teenager and two police officers.
  • December 9, 1965, Kecksburg, Pennsylvania: The Kecksburg UFO incident involved a large fireball seen over multiple states and Canada.
  • 1969, Leary, Georgia: Jimmy Carter claimed to have seen a UFO.
  • October 11, 1973, Pascagoula, Mississippi: The Pascagoula Abduction involved Charles Hickson and Calvin Parker claiming to have been abducted by aliens.
  • January 12, 1975, North Bergen, New Jersey: An incident occurred near the Stonehenge apartment building in North Bergen, New Jersey.
  • November 5, 1975, Snowflake, Arizona: Travis Walton claimed to have been abducted by a UFO.
  • December 17, 1977, Council Bluffs, Iowa: Local residents reported seeing a reddish fireball and recovered molten metal.
  • August 27, 1979, Stephen, Minnesota: Deputy Sheriff Val Johnson’s patrol car was struck by a blinding ball of light.
  • December 29, 1980, Dayton, Texas: The Cash-Landrum Incident involved a reported UFO sighting and subsequent health issues for the witnesses.
  • November 11, 1987, Gulf Breeze, Florida: The Gulf Breeze UFO incident occurred.
  • March 8, 1994, Multiple Locations, Michigan: Over 100 witnesses reported seeing objects with colored lights across West Michigan.
  • March 13, 1997, Phoenix, Arizona: The Phoenix Lights involved a series of optical phenomena seen by thousands of people. Former Arizona governor Fife Symington admitted to seeing a delta-shaped craft.
  • January 5, 2000, Highland, Illinois: The “St. Clair Triangle” sighting involved multiple police officers and other witnesses reporting a massive, silent, triangular aircraft.
  • August 21, 2004, Chicago, Illinois: A triangular formation of reddish lights was seen by hundreds of witnesses.
  • November 7, 2006, Chicago, Illinois: Employees at Chicago O’Hare International Airport reported seeing a metallic, saucer-shaped craft.
  • January 5, 2009, Morristown, New Jersey: Unidentified red objects were seen over Morristown, later revealed to be a hoax by Joe Rudy and Chris Russo.
  • February 19, 2018, Desert, Arizona: Commercial pilots saw an unidentified object, later attributed to a “high-altitude research balloon.”
  • February 21, 2021, Clayton, New Mexico: A pilot reported seeing a long cylindrical object.
  • February 10, 2023, Deadhorse, Alaska: A high-altitude object was shot down over the Beaufort Sea.
  • February 12, 2023, Lake Huron, Michigan: An octagonal object was shot down over Lake Huron.
