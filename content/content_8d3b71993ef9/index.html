List of Alleged UFO Sightings in the United States ðŸ”—

This is a comprehensive list of alleged UFO sightings in the United States, presented in a tabular format. Each entry includes the date, city, state, a link to a main article if available, and a brief description of the event.

Table of Sightings ðŸ”—

The table is the core of the article, detailing numerous alleged UFO sightings across the United States from 1865 to 2023. Key information includes:

  • September 1865, Cadotte Pass, Montana: Trapper James Lumley reported a craft crash with sulfur smells and hieroglyphics.
  • 1896-1897, Multiple Locations: Sightings of “mystery airships” across the US.
  • April 17, 1897, Aurora, Texas: Residents claimed an airship crashed, and the alien pilot was buried in the local cemetery.
  • February 24, 1942, Los Angeles, California: The “Battle of Los Angeles,” initially thought to be an enemy attack, later attributed to “war nerves” and a weather balloon. Secretary of the Navy Frank Knox called it a “false alarm.”
  • August 1945, Jornada del Muerto, New Mexico: Two boys reported an “avocado-shaped” object crashing with insect-like alien creatures. This is known as the Trinity UFO Case.
  • June 21, 1947, Maury Island, Washington: An incident reported before the Kenneth Arnold sighting, involving alleged encounters with “men in black.”
  • June 24, 1947, Mount Rainier, Washington: Kenneth Arnold reported seeing nine unusual objects flying in formation, describing them as “erratic” and moving at “tremendous speed.”
  • July 1947, Roswell, New Mexico: The Roswell UFO Incident, involving the recovery of materials that became the subject of intense speculation. The US military claims it was a research balloon, while ufologists believe it was an alien craft.
  • January 7, 1948, Maysville, Kentucky: The Mantell Incident, where Captain Thomas F. Mantell, a Kentucky Air National Guard pilot, crashed and died while pursuing a UFO.
  • July 24, 1948, Montgomery, Alabama: The Chiles-Whitted UFO Encounter, where two commercial pilots reported their Douglas DC-3 nearly collided with a strange torpedo-shaped object.
  • October 1, 1948, Fargo, North Dakota: The Gorman Dogfight, where a US Air Force pilot sighted and pursued a UFO for 27 minutes.
  • May 11, 1950, McMinnville, Oregon: The McMinnville UFO photographs, taken by Paul and Evelyn Trent.
  • May 29, 1950, Mount Vernon, Maryland: Captain Willis Sperry and crew reported a “brilliant, diffused, bluish light.”
  • August 15, 1950, Great Falls, Montana: The Mariana UFO incident, which garnered national media attention.
  • August 25, 1951, Lubbock, Texas: The Lubbock Lights, a V-shaped formation of lights seen over the town.
  • May 24, 1952, Burbank, California: Orfeo Angelucci claimed to encounter flying saucers and friendly pilots.
  • July 13, 1952, Washington, D.C.: The 1952 Washington D.C. UFO incident, a series of unidentified flying object reports.
  • September 12, 1952, Flatwoods, West Virginia: Six boys and a woman reported a UFO landing and a bizarre-looking creature, known as the Flatwoods monster.
  • November 23, 1953, Sault Ste. Marie, Michigan: Felix Moncla, Jr., a US Air Force pilot, disappeared with 2nd Lt. Robert Wilson while pursuing a UFO over Lake Superior.
  • November 2, 1957, Levelland, Texas: The Levelland UFO case, where multiple residents reported seeing a UFO.
  • September 19, 1961, Lancaster, New Hampshire: Betty and Barney Hill claimed to have been abducted by extraterrestrials, known as the Hill Abduction.
  • April 24, 1964, Socorro, New Mexico: Lonnie Zamora, a New Mexico police officer, reported a UFO close encounter.
  • September 3, 1965, Exeter, New Hampshire: The Exeter incident, where a large UFO was seen by a teenager and two police officers.
  • December 9, 1965, Kecksburg, Pennsylvania: The Kecksburg UFO incident, where a large fireball was seen, and metal debris was reported.
  • 1969, Leary, Georgia: Jimmy Carter claimed to have seen a UFO.
  • October 11, 1973, Pascagoula, Mississippi: The Pascagoula Abduction, where Charles Hickson and Calvin Parker claimed to have been abducted by aliens.
  • January 12, 1975, North Bergen, New Jersey: The Stonehenge incident, reported near the Stonehenge apartment building.
  • November 5, 1975, Snowflake, Arizona: Travis Walton claimed to have been abducted by a UFO.
  • December 17, 1977, Council Bluffs, Iowa: Residents reported a reddish fireball and a grass fire with molten metal.
  • August 27, 1979, Stephen, Minnesota: The Val Johnson incident, where Deputy Sheriff Val Johnson’s patrol car was struck by a ball of light.
  • December 29, 1980, Dayton, Texas: The Cash-Landrum Incident, where witnesses claimed health damage after a UFO sighting.
  • November 11, 1987, Gulf Breeze, Florida: The Gulf Breeze UFO incident.
  • March 8, 1994, Multiple Locations, Michigan: Over 100 witnesses reported multiple objects across West Michigan, known as the 1994 Lake Michigan UFO event.
  • March 13, 1997, Phoenix, Arizona: The Phoenix Lights, a series of optical phenomena seen by thousands. Former Arizona governor Fife Symington admitted to seeing a delta-shaped craft.
  • January 5, 2000, Highland, Illinois: The “St. Clair Triangle” sighting, where police officers and other witnesses reported a massive, silent, triangular aircraft.
  • August 21, 2004, Chicago, Illinois: A triangular formation of reddish lights was seen by hundreds of witnesses.
  • November 7, 2006, Chicago, Illinois: The Chicago O’Hare UFO sighting, where airport employees witnessed a metallic, saucer-shaped craft.
  • January 5, 2009, Morristown, New Jersey: Five unidentified red objects were seen, later revealed to be a hoax by Joe Rudy and Chris Russo.
  • February 19, 2018, Desert, Arizona: Commercial pilots saw an unidentified object, likely a “high-altitude research balloon.”
  • February 21, 2021, Clayton, New Mexico: A pilot reported seeing a long cylindrical object.
  • February 10, 2023, Deadhorse, Alaska: A high-altitude object was shot down by the U.S. Air Force.
  • February 12, 2023, Lake Huron, Michigan: An octagonal object was shot down by the US Air Force.
