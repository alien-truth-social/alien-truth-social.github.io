This is a compilation of reported UFO sightings within the United States, presented in a table format. Each entry includes the date, city, state, a link to a main article if available, and a brief description of the event.

Sightings ðŸ”—

This section details specific instances of alleged UFO sightings, arranged chronologically.

  • September 1865, Cadotte Pass, Montana: Trapper James Lumley reported a craft crash, describing sulfur smells and hieroglyphics.

  • 1896â€“1897, Multiple Locations: Sightings of airship-like vehicles were reported across the United States, known as the Mystery airships.

  • April 17, 1897, Aurora, Texas: Residents claimed an airship crashed, and the alien pilot was buried in the local cemetery, known as the Aurora, Texas, UFO incident.

  • February 24, 1942, Los Angeles, California: The “Battle of Los Angeles” involved an alleged enemy attack and anti-aircraft artillery barrage. Initially thought to be from Japan, it was later attributed to “war nerves” and a lost weather balloon. Key figures include Secretary of the Navy Frank Knox.

  • August 1945, Jornada del Muerto, New Mexico: Two boys reported an “avocado-shaped” object crashing, with insect-like alien creatures inside, known as the Trinity UFO Case.

  • June 21, 1947, Maury Island, Washington: An incident occurred before the Kenneth Arnold sighting, involving an alleged encounter with “men in black.”

  • June 24, 1947, Mount Rainier, Washington: Kenneth Arnold reported seeing nine unusual objects flying in formation, describing them as bright and moving at “tremendous speed.”

  • July 1947, Roswell, New Mexico: The Roswell UFO Incident involved the recovery of materials near Roswell, sparking speculation about a crashed alien craft versus a top-secret research balloon.

  • January 7, 1948, Maysville, Kentucky: The Mantell Incident involved the death of Captain Thomas F. Mantell, a Kentucky Air National Guard pilot, while pursuing a UFO. Historian David Michael Jacobs noted this case marked a shift in public and governmental perceptions of UFOs.

  • July 24, 1948, Montgomery, Alabama: The Chiles-Whitted UFO Encounter involved two commercial pilots reporting a near collision with a strange torpedo-shaped object.

  • October 1, 1948, Fargo, North Dakota: The Gorman Dogfight involved a US Air Force pilot sighting and pursuing a UFO for 27 minutes.

  • May 11, 1950, McMinnville, Oregon: The McMinnville UFO photographs were taken by Paul and Evelyn Trent on a farm.

  • May 29, 1950, Mount Vernon, Maryland: Captain Willis Sperry and crewmembers reported a “brilliant, diffused, bluish light.”

  • August 15, 1950, Great Falls, Montana: The Mariana UFO incident garnered national media attention.

  • August 25, 1951, Lubbock, Texas: The Lubbock Lights were a v-shaped formation of lights seen over the town.

  • May 24, 1952, Burbank, California: Orfeo Angelucci claimed to encounter flying saucers and friendly pilots during his drives home.

  • July 13, 1952, Washington, D.C.: The 1952 Washington, D.C. UFO incident involved multiple reports of unidentified flying objects over the city.

  • September 12, 1952, Flatwoods, West Virginia: Six boys and a woman reported seeing a UFO landing and a bizarre-looking creature, known as the Flatwoods monster.

  • November 23, 1953, Sault Ste. Marie, Michigan: Felix Moncla, Jr., a US Air Force pilot, disappeared with 2nd Lt. Robert Wilson while pursuing a UFO over Lake Superior.

  • November 2, 1957, Levelland, Texas: The Levelland UFO case involved multiple sightings of a UFO in the small town.

  • September 19, 1961, Lancaster, New Hampshire: Betty and Barney Hill claimed to have been abducted by extraterrestrials, known as the Betty and Barney Hill abduction.

  • April 24, 1964, Socorro, New Mexico: Lonnie Zamora, a New Mexico police officer, reported a UFO close encounter, known as the Lonnie Zamora incident.

  • September 3, 1965, Exeter, New Hampshire: The Exeter incident involved a large UFO seen by a teenager and two police officers.

  • December 9, 1965, Kecksburg, Pennsylvania: The Kecksburg UFO incident involved a large fireball seen over multiple states and Ontario, Canada.

  • 1969, Leary, Georgia: Jimmy Carter claimed to have seen a UFO.

  • October 11, 1973, Pascagoula, Mississippi: Charles Hickson and Calvin Parker reported being abducted, known as the Pascagoula Abduction.

  • January 12, 1975, North Bergen, New Jersey: An incident occurred near the Stonehenge apartment building, known as the Stonehenge incident.

  • November 5, 1975, Snowflake, Arizona: Travis Walton claimed to have been abducted by a UFO, known as the Travis Walton UFO incident.

  • December 17, 1977, Council Bluffs, Iowa: Residents reported a reddish fireball and a grass fire with molten metal.

  • August 27, 1979, Stephen, Minnesota: Deputy Sheriff Val Johnson’s patrol car was struck by a ball of light, known as the Val Johnson incident.

  • December 29, 1980, Dayton, Texas: The Cash-Landrum Incident involved a UFO sighting and subsequent health issues for the witnesses.

  • November 11, 1987, Gulf Breeze, Florida: The Gulf Breeze UFO incident occurred.

  • March 8, 1994, Michigan: Over 100 witnesses reported multiple objects, known as the 1994 Lake Michigan UFO event.

  • March 13, 1997, Phoenix, Arizona: The Phoenix Lights involved a series of optical phenomena seen by thousands of people. Former Arizona governor Fife Symington admitted to seeing a delta-shaped craft.

  • January 5, 2000, Highland, Illinois: The “St. Clair Triangle” sighting involved multiple police officers and witnesses reporting a massive, silent, triangular aircraft.

  • August 21, 2004, Chicago, Illinois: A triangular formation of reddish lights was seen by hundreds of witnesses.

  • November 7, 2006, Chicago, Illinois: Employees at Chicago O’Hare International Airport reported a metallic, saucer-shaped craft, known as the Chicago O’Hare UFO sighting 2006.

  • January 5, 2009, Morristown, New Jersey: Five unidentified red objects were seen. Joe Rudy and Chris Russo later admitted it was a hoax, known as the Morristown UFO.

  • February 19, 2018, Arizona Desert: Commercial pilots saw an unidentified object, later attributed to a “high-altitude research balloon.”

  • February 21, 2021, Clayton, New Mexico: A pilot reported seeing a long cylindrical object.

  • February 10, 2023, Deadhorse, Alaska: A high-altitude object was shot down over the Beaufort Sea, known as the 2023 Alaska high-altitude object.

  • February 12, 2023, Lake Huron, Michigan: An octagonal object was shot down over Lake Huron, known as the 2023 Lake Huron high-altitude object.
