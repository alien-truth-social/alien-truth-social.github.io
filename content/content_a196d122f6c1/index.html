List of Alleged Extraterrestrial Beings ðŸ”—

This Wikipedia entry catalogues various extraterrestrial beings that have been reported in alleged close encounters, claimed sightings, or speculated to be associated with unidentified flying objects (UFOs). It clarifies that the term “extraterrestrial being” in this context differs from the biological definition of “alien species” in ecology.

List ðŸ”—

This section presents a table listing alleged extraterrestrial beings, providing their names and brief descriptions.

  • Flatwoods Monster: A tall humanoid with a spade-shaped head, associated with the Flatwoods Monster incident.
  • Greys: Grey-skinned humanoids, typically around 1 meter tall, hairless, with large heads and black almond-shaped eyes. They are linked to the Roswell incident and have been a prominent feature in alleged alien contact since the 1960s.
  • Hopkinsville Goblin: Small, greenish-silver humanoids, connected to the Kellyâ€“Hopkinsville encounter.
  • Little Green Men: Diminutive green humanoids, a classic cultural stereotype, though actual abduction reports rarely match this description.
  • Nordic Aliens: Humanoids with stereotypical “Nordic” features (tall, blonde hair, blue eyes), sometimes referred to as Space Brothers. This category includes Plejarens (previously known as Pleiadeans), Venusians, Tall Whites, and Agarthans from Agartha.
  • Cryptozoological Animals and Cryptobotanical Plants: Creatures from folklore, religion, and mythology (e.g., golems, dwarves, giants from Atlantis), as well as reports of ghosts, poltergeists, and time travelers. Examples include the Mothman, Jersey Devil, Loch Ness Monster, Nommo, Mokele-mbembe, Yokai, Quinametzin, Rephaite, and Loveland Frogman. It suggests that some believe these creatures are of extraterrestrial or mixed origin, as proposed in the extraterrestrial, interdimensional, or cryptoterrestrial hypotheses.
  • Reptilians and Reptiloids: Tall, scaly humanoids, sometimes linked to ancient astronauts. This category includes Draconians and Orion reptilian-humanoids. David Icke is mentioned as a proponent of the reptilian conspiracy theory.
  • Rods or Skyfish: Elongated visual artifacts appearing in photos and videos, sometimes claimed to be extraterrestrial beings, but generally believed to be caused by motion blur from flying insects.
