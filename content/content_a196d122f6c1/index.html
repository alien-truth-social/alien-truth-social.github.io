List of Alleged Extraterrestrial Beings ðŸ”—

This Wikipedia entry catalogues various extraterrestrial beings reported in close encounters, claimed sightings, or speculated to be associated with unidentified flying objects (UFOs). It clarifies that the term “extraterrestrial being” in this context differs from the biological definition of “alien species” in ecology.

List ðŸ”—

This section presents a table outlining alleged extraterrestrial beings, providing their names, descriptions, and associated events or concepts.

  • Flatwoods Monster: A tall humanoid with a spade-shaped head, linked to the Flatwoods Monster incident.
  • Greys: Grey-skinned humanoids, typically around 1 meter tall, hairless, with large heads and black almond-shaped eyes. They are associated with the Roswell incident and are the most common type of extraterrestrial being reported since the 1960s.
  • Hopkinsville Goblin: Small, greenish-silver humanoids, connected to the Kellyâ€“Hopkinsville encounter.
  • Little Green Men: Diminutive green humanoids, a classic cultural stereotype, though actual abduction reports rarely match this description.
  • Nordic Aliens: Humanoids with stereotypical “Nordic” features (tall, blonde hair, blue eyes). They are sometimes called Space Brothers and include groups like the Plejaren (previously known as Pleiadeans), Venusians, Tall Whites, and Agarthans from Agartha.
  • Cryptozoological Animals and Cryptobotanical Plants: Creatures from folklore, religion, and mythology (e.g., golems, dwarves, giants from Atlantis), as well as reports of ghosts, poltergeists, and time travelers. Examples include the Mothman, Jersey Devil, Loch Ness Monster, Nommo, Mokele-mbembe, Yokai, Quinametzin, Rephaite, and Loveland Frogman. Some believe these creatures are of extraterrestrial or mixed origin, as suggested by the extraterrestrial, interdimensional, or cryptoterrestrial hypotheses.
  • Reptilians and Reptiloids: Tall, scaly humanoids, sometimes linked to ancient astronauts. They include Draconians and Orion reptilian-humanoid matriarchies. David Icke is a prominent advocate of the reptilian conspiracy theory.
  • Rods or Skyfish: Elongated visual artifacts in photos and videos, sometimes claimed to be extraterrestrial beings, but generally believed to be motion blur from flying insects.
