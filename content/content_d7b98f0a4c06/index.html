“Aliens Exist” is a song by the American rock band Blink-182, featured on their 1999 album Enema of the State. The song, primarily written by guitarist Tom DeLonge with additional credit to bassist Mark Hoppus, is a lighthearted take on the existence of extraterrestrial life. It references UFO phenomena, including alleged CIA involvement and the Majestic 12.

Background ðŸ”—

Blink-182, originating from suburban San Diego, gained prominence in the late 1990s with their pop-punk sound. The band members included guitarist Tom DeLonge, bassist Mark Hoppus, and drummer Travis Barker. DeLonge’s fascination with extraterrestrials began in middle school and continued as he toured, reading books about paranormal activity. Barker recalls DeLonge constantly searching for UFOs. DeLonge used his first personal computer to research alien phenomena. He amassed a collection of UFO books and military interviews about UFO experiences. Hoppus found DeLonge’s beliefs amusing. DeLonge and Hoppus often debated the song’s subject matter, leaving each other messages on their equipment. Barker, then a touring musician, arranged the song’s tempo and structure.

Composition ðŸ”—

The song is written in common time with a tempo of 216 beats per minute, in the key of B major. The lyrics, written by DeLonge, express youthful curiosity about the universe and attempt to convince listeners of the legitimacy of his claims. The lyrics suggest the CIA is suppressing information and reference the Majestic 12, a supposed group of scientists studying alien spacecraft in the 1940s. DeLonge explained the song’s perspective as that of someone who believes in aliens but is dismissed by others, directing his frustration towards the government.

Release and Reception ðŸ”—

“Aliens Exist” was initially previewed as part of an online medley and officially released on Enema of the State in June 1999. It received positive reviews, with some critics praising its departure from typical pop-punk themes.

Live Performances ðŸ”—

Although not released as a single, the band performed the song on The Tonight Show with Jay Leno in 2000. It was a regular part of their tour and live album, The Mark, Tom and Travis Show. The band retired the song in 2002 but revived it on their 2019 tour, with Matt Skiba filling in for DeLonge. DeLonge also performed the song with Angels & Airwaves in 2019. Following DeLonge’s return to Blink-182, the band continued to perform the song on their 2023 world tour, incorporating tabloid imagery of DeLonge’s interest in aliens.

Legacy ðŸ”—

The song has been re-examined in light of DeLonge’s continued pursuit of UFO research. It is remembered as a standout track from Enema of the State and is considered part of American pop culture’s fascination with aliens. Blink-182 embraced this image, using alien imagery on merchandise and at concerts. DeLonge continued to explore alien themes in his band Angels & Airwaves. Machine Gun Kelly’s “Concert for Aliens” was seen as reminiscent of DeLonge’s earlier work. DeLonge co-founded To the Stars, a company focused on aerospace, science, and entertainment, with former government and intelligence officials. In 2017, the company released leaked military footage of unidentified aerial phenomena, later confirmed by the Pentagon, which legitimized DeLonge’s research. This led to the Pentagon formally establishing interest in studying UFOs.
