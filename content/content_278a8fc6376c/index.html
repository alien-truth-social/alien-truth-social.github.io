This article discusses the Test Tube Aliens toy line, detailing their story, features, and functionality.

Story ðŸ”—

The narrative behind Test Tube Aliens involves two factions of aliens: the “good” aliens from the dying planet Naratuko seeking a new home, and the “evil” aliens responsible for their planet’s destruction, pursuing them across the universe. The good aliens reverted to a pre-birth state to escape, sacrificing strength for intelligence. Later, the storyline was altered to reveal that all the original aliens were evil, with the “true good” aliens being the X series. The evil aliens aimed to contaminate water sources after being discarded. This was followed by the release of “Test Tube Aliens: Pure Evil,” featuring entirely evil aliens.

The toy itself ðŸ”—

The toy consists of a test tube containing a “chrysalis” which, when water is added, dissolves to reveal an infant alien. The alien’s “heartbeat” is indicated by flashing lights. The alien requires feeding with “Sloog” (a powder mixed with water) initially, then tap water. It needs 12 hours of light and 12 hours of darkness daily to remain “healthy,” with the toy indicating if water levels are incorrect. If not cared for properly, the alien will “die.” The toy also interacted with a now-defunct website where users could check the alien’s health and induce coma or panic modes. A picture of Kurion, a good alien, is included.

How it works ðŸ”—

The alien’s body contains Polyacrylamide, a polymer that expands when it absorbs water. “Sloog” contains a weak acid that slows this expansion. Electrodes on the alien’s antenna and head sense the acid and water level, determining the alien’s “health.” A light detector in the alien’s head responds to flashes from the Test Tube Aliens website (no longer active) and tracks light/dark exposure.
