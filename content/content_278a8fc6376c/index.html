This entry discusses the Test Tube Aliens toy line, detailing its backstory, the toy’s features, how it functions, and providing references and external links.

Story ðŸ”—

The narrative revolves around two factions of aliens: the “good” aliens fleeing their dying homeworld of Naratuko and the “evil” aliens responsible for its destruction, pursuing them across the universe. The good aliens genetically revert to a pre-birth state to escape, seeking a sympathetic lifeform to restore them. A later development reveals that all the original aliens are evil, with the “good” aliens plotting to contaminate Earth’s water sources after being discarded. This leads to the release of a “Pure Evil” line of Test Tube Aliens.

The toy itself ðŸ”—

The toy consists of a test tube containing a white cocoon or chrysalis. Adding water dissolves the cocoon, revealing an infant alien. The alien’s “heartbeat” is indicated by flashing lights. The alien requires feeding with “Sloog” and must be kept in a regular cycle of daylight and darkness to remain “healthy.” The alien can detect water levels and will signal if they are too high or too low. If not properly cared for, the alien will “die.” The toy also interacts with a now-defunct website where users could induce coma or panic modes and check the alien’s age and health. A picture of Kurion, a good alien, is included.

How it works ðŸ”—

The alien’s body contains Polyacrylamide, a polymer that expands when it absorbs water. The “Sloog” contains a weak acid that slows the expansion, making the growth appear gradual. Electrodes on the alien’s antenna and head sense the presence of the acid and measure water levels to determine if the alien is “healthy.” A light detector in the alien’s head responds to flashes from the website, tracking light and dark exposure. The Test Tube Alien website is currently offline.
