Pentagon UFO Videos ðŸ”—

This article discusses the widely publicized cockpit instrumentation display videos from US Navy fighter jets, often characterized as UFOs.

Background ðŸ”—

This section provides context for the videos, referencing historical UFO photographs and events. It details the initial incident on November 14, 2004, involving Commander David Fravor of the USS Nimitz Carrier Strike Group investigating radar indications of a possible target off the coast of southern California. The USS Princeton had been tracking unusual aircraft for two weeks prior to the incident. Fravor’s account of encountering a white, oval object hovering above an ocean disturbance is highlighted, as well as the observations of other pilots. It also mentions Lieutenant Commander Chad Underwood, who recorded the “FLIR” video and coined the term “Tic Tac” to describe the object. The section also mentions the 2014-2015 incidents involving fighter pilots associated with the USS Theodore Roosevelt carrier strike group operating off the East Coast of the United States, during which the GIMBAL and GOFAST videos were recorded.

Release of Videos ðŸ”—

This section focuses on the public release of the videos. The New York Times reported on the incidents and published the “FLIR” and “GIMBAL” videos in December 2017, while the Washington Post published the “GOFAST” video. Christopher Mellon, former Deputy Assistant Secretary of Defense for Intelligence, provided the videos to the press. Luis Elizondo, the director of the Advanced Aerospace Threat Identification Program, resigned from the Pentagon in October 2017 to protest government secrecy. In September 2019, the Pentagon confirmed the videos were made by naval aviators. On April 27, 2020, the Pentagon formally released the three videos. In February 2020, the United States Navy confirmed that intelligence briefings have been provided to members of Congress.

2021 Release ðŸ”—

This section details the release of additional footage in 2021. In April 2021, the Pentagon confirmed the authenticity of a video taken by Navy personnel aboard USS Russell in 2019, showing a triangular object. Mick West suggested the image was the result of an optical effect called a bokeh. The Pentagon also confirmed photographs of objects described as “sphere”, “acorn” and “metallic blimp”. In May 2021, a second video recorded by Navy personnel aboard the USS Omaha on July 15, 2019, was confirmed and is under review by the UAP Task Force. The video purportedly shows a spherical object flying over the ocean. Mick West commented on the video, suggesting it could simply be a plane.

2023 Release ðŸ”—

This section covers the release of additional videos in 2023. In early 2023, an object was filmed by two MQ-9 drones in South Asia, initially believed to be anomalous but later identified as a commuter aircraft. On May 31, 2023, Sean M. Kirkpatrick shared an infrared video recorded in the western United States in 2021, which AARO concluded to be commercial aircraft. On April 19, 2023, the Pentagon released another video featuring MQ-9 drone footage from the Middle East depicting an unidentified aerial phenomenon resembling a small “metallic orb”. Sean M. Kirkpatrick briefed a Senate Armed Services subcommittee on the video, but stated they are unable to fully identify anything from the video.

Potential Explanations ðŸ”—

This section explores potential explanations for the aerial phenomena recorded in the videos. As of 2020, the Department of Defense characterizes the phenomena as “unidentified”. Skeptical explanations include instrument malfunction, human observational error, or common aircraft. Mick West cautioned that believers in “alien disclosure” are “encroaching on these real issues of UAPs”. Adam Frank speculated that the UFOs could be drones deployed by rivals like Russia and China. Thomas Bania speculated that they could be some form of electronic warfare fielded by China or Russia. The US Navy updated its reporting guidelines for unexplained aerial observations. Senator Marco Rubio expressed concern that the UFOs may be Chinese or Russian technology. Admiral Gary Roughead said that most assessments were inconclusive. Jeff Wise suggests advancements in electronic warfare could deceive sensors. In 2023, David Fravor testified under oath regarding the USS Nimitz sighting, stating that the technology was far superior to anything the US had.

June 2021 UFO Report ðŸ”—

This section discusses the preliminary report on UAPs released by the US Office of the Director of National Intelligence (ODNI) on June 25, 2021. The report came to no conclusion about what the UAPs were, citing a lack of sufficient data. Some UAPs appeared to exhibit unusual flight characteristics, including high velocity and maneuverability. The report indicated that the UAP recordings probably were of physical objects and proposed five possible categories of explanation: airborne clutter, natural atmospheric phenomena, US government or industry development technology, foreign craft, and an “Other” category. The report raised concerns about safety and security issues and indicated that investigation of the topic would continue.

FOI Request by The Black Vault ðŸ”—

This section details a Freedom of Information request made by The Black Vault for the release of more video footage. The government confirmed it had more footage but refused to release it, citing concerns for national security. Gregory Cason stated that the release of this information would harm national security.

In Popular Culture ðŸ”—

This section notes the videos’ appearance in the History Channel series Unidentified: Inside America’s UFO Investigation and an interview with Fravor on The Joe Rogan Experience.

This section provides a list of related topics, including the Advanced Aerospace Threat Identification Program, Unidentified Aerial Phenomena Task Force, All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office, Navy UFO patents, and the film The Phenomenon.
