Roswell, New Mexico ðŸ”—

Roswell is a city located in Chaves County, New Mexico, serving as its county seat. It’s known for the alleged Roswell incident and is home to the New Mexico Military Institute (NMMI) and an Eastern New Mexico University campus.

History ðŸ”—

The area was first settled by pioneers from Missouri in 1865, though they abandoned the site due to lack of water. Van C. Smith and Aaron Wilburn established a general store and post office in 1869, which marked the beginning of the modern city. Smith named the town after his father, Roswell Smith. Captain Joseph Calloway Lea and his family acquired the land in 1877. The discovery of a major aquifer in 1890 and the arrival of the Pecos Valley Railroad in 1892 spurred growth. During World War II, a prisoner-of-war camp was located nearby, with German POWs contributing to infrastructure projects. Walker Air Force Base was important from 1941 to 1967, after which Roswell transitioned into a retirement community. The city has capitalized on the Roswell UFO incident, attracting tourists interested in UFOs and aliens. In 2024, Roswell was chosen to host the Reno Air Races starting in 2025. Felix Baumgartner’s record-breaking skydive occurred in Roswell in 2012.

Geography ðŸ”—

Roswell is situated in the High Great Plains of southeastern New Mexico, west of the Pecos River. U.S. Routes 70, 285, and 380 intersect in the city, providing connections to other cities like Clovis, Alamogordo, Santa Fe, Carlsbad, Brownfield, and Socorro. The city’s total area is approximately 29.776 square miles, with a small percentage covered by water.

Education ðŸ”—

Public schools are managed by the Roswell Independent School District, which includes Goddard High School and Roswell High School. Private schools include All Saints Catholic School and Gateway Christian School. Higher education institutions include Eastern New Mexico University-Roswell and New Mexico Military Institute.

Sports ðŸ”—

The Roswell Invaders play in the Pecos League of professional baseball. Joe Bauman set a minor-league record of 72 home runs for the Roswell Rockets in 1954. Willie Stargell played for the Roswell Pirates in 1959.

Transportation ðŸ”—

Roswell Air Center is served by American Airlines via American Eagle. Pecos Trails Transit provides bus routes. Greyhound Lines offers intercity services. Major highways include U.S. Route 70, U.S. Route 285, and U.S. Route 380. BNSF Railway provides freight services.

Local Industry ðŸ”—

Leprino Foods, one of the world’s largest mozzarella factories, is located in Roswell. The city was also home to the Transportation Manufacturing Corporation factory, known for producing RTS city buses.

Cultural Attractions ðŸ”—

Roswell’s tourism industry revolves around aerospace engineering and ufology museums and businesses, with alien-themed iconography. The International UFO Museum and Research Center is a major attraction. An annual UFO festival has been held since 1995. The Roswell Museum maintains an exhibit on Robert H. Goddard’s rocket engine development. The Anderson Museum of Contemporary Art features works by artists from the Roswell Artist-in-Residence program.
