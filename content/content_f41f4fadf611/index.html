This article discusses the 1995 pseudo-documentary Alien Autopsy: Fact or Fiction, which featured footage purporting to show an alien autopsy conducted after the Roswell incident. The program, hosted by Jonathan Frakes, aired on Fox and Channel 4, sparking intense debate before being thoroughly debunked.

Production ðŸ”—

This section details the production of the hoaxed footage. Ray Santilli and Gary Shoefield admitted to creating the film in a 2006 documentary. The autopsy was simulated in a London flat, with artist John Humphreys constructing the alien bodies from plaster casts filled with offal, sheep brains, and raspberry jam. Humphreys also played the role of the chief examiner. Alien artifacts were also fabricated. A homeless man was paid to pose as the original cameraman.

In popular culture ðŸ”—

This section covers the reception and parody of Alien Autopsy in media. The X-Files satirized the footage, and Fox later aired a special debunking it. A fictionalized version of the film’s creation was retold in the comedy film Alien Autopsy (2006).
