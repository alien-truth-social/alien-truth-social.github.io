This article discusses the 1995 pseudo-documentary Alien Autopsy: Fact or Fiction, which featured footage purporting to show an alien autopsy conducted after the Roswell incident. The program, hosted by Jonathan Frakes, aired on Fox and other networks worldwide, sparking intense debate before being thoroughly debunked as a hoax.

Production ðŸ”—

This section details the production of the Alien Autopsy footage. Ray Santilli and Gary Shoefield admitted to creating the footage in a 2006 documentary. The simulated autopsy was filmed in a London living room using fabricated alien bodies constructed by artist John Humphreys. The alien bodies were made of plaster casts filled with offal, sheep brains, and raspberry jam. Alien artifacts were also created by Humphreys. A homeless man was paid to pose as the original cameraman.

In popular culture ðŸ”—

This section outlines how Alien Autopsy was received and referenced in popular culture. The program was widely derided, including being satirized in The X-Files. Fox later aired a special debunking the footage. A fictionalized version of the creation and release of the footage was retold in the comedy film Alien Autopsy (2006).
