This article discusses the 1995 pseudo-documentary Alien Autopsy: Fact or Fiction, which featured footage purporting to show an alien autopsy conducted after the Roswell incident. The article distinguishes this footage from the 2006 film Alien Autopsy, which is a fictionalized account of the events surrounding the creation and release of the footage.

Production ðŸ”—

This section details the production of the hoaxed alien autopsy footage. In 2006, Ray Santilli and Gary Shoefield admitted to creating the 1995 footage. They filmed a simulated autopsy on a fabricated alien in a set constructed in a London living room. Artist and sculptor John Humphreys constructed two dummy alien bodies filled with raspberry jam, sheep brains, and chicken entrails. Humphreys also played the role of the chief examiner. Alien artifacts were also created by Humphreys. Santilli and Shoefield also admitted to finding a homeless man in Los Angeles and paying him to play the role of the cameraman.

In popular culture ðŸ”—

This section discusses how Alien Autopsy was received and satirized in the media. The X-Files featured alien autopsy footage that was decried as a hoax. Fox aired a special debunking the 1995 footage. A fictionalized version of the creation of the footage was retold in the comedy film Alien Autopsy (2006).
