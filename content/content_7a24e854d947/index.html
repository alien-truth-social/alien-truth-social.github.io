Ufology ðŸ”—

Ufology is the investigation of unidentified flying objects (UFOs) by individuals who believe they may have extraordinary origins, often extraterrestrial. While governments, private groups, and fringe science have investigated UFOs, ufology is generally considered pseudoscience by skeptics and science educators.

Etymology ðŸ”—

The term “ufology” is a neologism derived from “UFO” and the suffix “-logy.” Edward J. Ruppelt is credited with coining the term UFO. Early uses of “ufology” appeared in a 1957 Fantastic Universe article and a 1958 presentation for The Planetary Center.

Historical Background ðŸ”—

Ufology’s roots can be traced to several historical phenomena:

  • Mystery Airships (late 1890s): Unexplained aerial objects reported during this period.
  • Foo Fighters (World War II): Reports from Allied airmen of unusual objects during flight.
  • Ghost Fliers (1930s): Sightings in Europe and North America.
  • Ghost Rockets (1946): Sightings in Scandinavia, particularly Sweden.
  • Kenneth Arnold Sighting (1947): Arnold’s report of “flying saucers” popularized the concept.

Increased media attention to UFOs after World War II coincided with the Cold War and concerns about Soviet aircraft. Official government involvement in ufology in the United States ended in the late 1960s after the Condon Committee report and the termination of Project Blue Book. Other countries, such as the United Kingdom, Canada, Denmark, Italy, and Sweden, also ended government-sponsored UFO activities. France is an exception, maintaining the GEIPAN program. In September 2023, NASA appointed Mark McInerney as the Director of U.A.P. to study such occurrences scientifically and transparently.

Status as a Pseudoscience ðŸ”—

Ufology is not recognized as a scientific field by academia and is generally considered a pseudoscience. Pseudoscience lacks adherence to the scientific method, supporting evidence, plausibility, or falsifiability. Social factors contribute to this status, including mistrust between ufology, the sciences, and government investigative bodies. Poor science communication regarding the extraterrestrial intelligence (ETI) hypothesis also hinders public understanding and academic inquiry.

Current Interest ðŸ”—

Interest in ufology persists. In 2021, Avi Loeb launched The Galileo Project to collect scientific evidence of extraterrestrial technology. The University of WÃ¼rzburg in Germany is developing sensors to detect and analyze aerial objects. A 2021 Gallup poll indicated increased belief among Americans that some UFOs are extraterrestrial spacecraft. In 2022, NASA announced a study to investigate UAP. In 2023, the RAND Corporation published a study reviewing 101,151 public reports of UAP sightings in the United States from 1998 to 2022.

Methodological Issues ðŸ”—

Scientific UFO research faces challenges due to the unpredictable nature of the phenomena, lack of reproducibility, and absence of tangible physicality. Most UFO sightings have mundane explanations, limiting the interpretive power of extraordinary events. Carl Sagan noted that reliable cases are uninteresting, and interesting cases are unreliable. J. Allen Hynek and Jacques VallÃ©e developed descriptive systems for characterizing UFO sightings.

Phenomena Linked to Ufology ðŸ”—

Certain phenomena are of interest to some ufologists:

  • Crop circles
  • Cattle mutilations
  • Anomalous materials
  • Alien abductions and implants

Some ufologists promote UFO conspiracy theories, such as the Roswell Incident and Majestic 12 documents. Robert Sheaffer has accused ufology of a “credulity explosion,” while James E. McDonald identified “cultism” as negatively impacting ufology.

In Posadism ðŸ”—

During the Cold War, ufology was synthesized with the ideas of Posadism, a Trotskyist movement in South America. Juan Posadas, the main theorist, believed humanity should appeal to extraterrestrials to intervene and collaborate in suppressing poverty and creating a socialist system on Earth.

Governmental and Private Ufology Studies ðŸ”—

Starting in the 1940s, governmental agencies and private groups sponsored investigations, studies, and conferences related to ufology. These studies include Project Sign, Project Magnet, Project Blue Book, the Robertson Panel, and the Condon Committee in the United States; the Flying Saucer Working Party and Project Condign in Britain; GEIPAN in France; and Project Hessdalen in Norway. Private studies include those by the RAND Corporation, Harvey Rutledge of the University of Missouri, and the National Press Club’s Disclosure Project. The United Nations sponsored meetings and hearings concerning UFO sightings from 1977 to 1979. In August 2020, the United States Department of Defense established the Unidentified Aerial Phenomena Task Force.

UFO Organizations and Events ðŸ”—

Numerous private organizations worldwide are dedicated to the study and discussion of ufology. Skeptic organizations also exist, emphasizing the pseudoscientific nature of ufology. During World UFO Day (July 2), ufologists raise public awareness of ufology.
