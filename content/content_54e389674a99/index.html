My Parents Are Aliens is a British children’s television sitcom created by Andy Watts and produced by Yorkshire Television/Granada Yorkshire. It aired on ITV from November 8, 1999, to December 18, 2006, spanning eight series. The show requires additional citations for verification.

Overview ðŸ”—

The show centers on the lives of orphaned siblings Mel, Josh, and Lucy Barker, who are fostered by Brian and Sophie Johnson. The children discover that their new parents are aliens from the planet Valux who crash-landed on Earth. The house they live in is a morphed form of their spaceship. Brian and Sophie have limited knowledge of Earth customs, and the children help them navigate human life while keeping their alien identities secret. The show’s humor is surreal and subversive, appealing to both children and older viewers.

Production ðŸ”—

The show was filmed in Studio 4 at The Leeds Studios. A laugh track was added from series 4 onwards. The last episode of series 7 featured the original cast and concluded ongoing plots. Series 8 introduced a new family, the Bennetts, after Brian and Sophie’s memories were erased. ITV closed its in-house children’s production unit, ending the show after series 8. Nickelodeon purchased the rights to make an American version, but the pilot never entered production.

Broadcast ðŸ”—

Series 1 to 7 was repeated regularly on CITV from the channel’s launch in 2006 up until late 2017, while series 8 repeats were discontinued by early 2010. Entertainment Rights Distribution handled worldwide distribution rights to the series.

Home media ðŸ”—

A VHS release containing all six Series One episodes was released in 2000 by Video Collection International and Granada Media. A single-release DVD containing two episodes from Series 2 and four from Series 3 titled The Best Of was released in 2006 by Right Entertainment/Universal Pictures Video UK. Right/Universal also released Series 1 and 2 in a box-set.

Awards and nominations ðŸ”—

The show won a Royal Television Society Award for Best Children’s Drama in 2000 and 2006. It was nominated for a Children’s BAFTA Award for Best Drama in 2001, 2003, and 2004. It also won Best Children’s Program at the Banff Television Festival and Best Children’s Fiction in 2002.
