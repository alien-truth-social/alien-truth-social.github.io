Ancient Astronauts ðŸ”—

Ancient astronauts, also known as ancient aliens, is a pseudoscientific belief that intelligent extraterrestrial beings visited Earth in ancient times and influenced the development of human civilization. Proponents suggest that these aliens affected cultures, technologies, religions, and even human biology. A common idea is that deities in various religions were actually extraterrestrials, and their advanced technologies were seen as divine powers by early humans. Academics and archaeologists dismiss these claims as pseudoarchaeology and unscientific, lacking credible peer-reviewed evidence. Some argue that these theories have racist undertones, diminishing the achievements of indigenous cultures.

Key Points:

  • Extraterrestrial contact in ancient times
  • Influence on human civilization
  • Deities as extraterrestrials
  • Dismissal by academics as pseudoscience
  • Potential racist implications

Overview ðŸ”—

Various terms are used to describe claims about ancient astronauts, including ancient aliens, ancient ufonauts, ancient space pilots, paleocontact, astronaut- or alien gods, and paleo- or biblical-SETI. Believers often assert that humans are descendants or creations of extraterrestrial intelligence that landed on Earth in the distant past. They propose that human knowledge, religion, and culture originated from these ancient alien visitors, who acted as a “mother culture.” Additionally, they claim that extraterrestrials built or assisted in building structures like the Egyptian pyramids and the Moai stone heads of Easter Island. Proponents cite documentary gaps in historical and archaeological records and point to archaeological artifacts they consider anachronistic and beyond the technical capabilities of ancient people. These artifacts, sometimes called “out-of-place artifacts,” include artwork and legends reinterpreted to support extraterrestrial contact or technologies.

Key Points:

  • Alternative terms for ancient astronaut theories
  • Humans as descendants or creations of extraterrestrials
  • Extraterrestrial origin of human knowledge and culture
  • Extraterrestrial involvement in building ancient structures
  • Use of “out-of-place artifacts” as evidence

Paleocontact narratives first appeared in early science fiction in the late 19th and early 20th centuries. Harold T. Wilkins proposed the idea in earnest in 1954. Erich von DÃ¤niken became a leading proponent of the ancient astronaut hypothesis in the late 1960s and early 1970s with his book Chariots of the Gods? He argued that certain artifacts required more advanced technology than was available to ancient cultures, suggesting they were constructed by extraterrestrials or humans who learned from them. These artifacts include Stonehenge, Pumapunku, the Moai of Easter Island, the Great Pyramid of Giza, and the ancient Baghdad electric batteries. Von DÃ¤niken interpreted ancient art and iconography as illustrations of air and space vehicles, non-human creatures, and advanced technology. He also suggested that geographically separated cultures shared artistic themes, implying a common extraterrestrial origin. He interpreted the sarcophagus lid of Maya ruler Pacal the Great as depicting an astronaut. He viewed the origins of many religions as reactions to encounters with an alien race, with humans considering alien technology supernatural and the aliens themselves as gods.

Zecharia Sitchin ðŸ”—

Zecharia Sitchin’s The Earth Chronicles series revolves around his interpretation of ancient Sumerian and Middle Eastern texts, megalithic sites, and artifacts. He hypothesized that the gods of ancient Mesopotamia were astronauts from the planet Nibiru, which he claimed the Sumerians believed to be a remote “12th planet.” Sitchin argued that Sumerian texts tell the story of the Anunnaki, inhabitants of Nibiru, who came to Earth approximately 400,000 years ago to mine raw materials, especially gold. They genetically engineered laborers, creating Homo sapiens sapiens, the Adapa or Adam of later mythology.

Key Points:

  • Sitchin’s interpretation of Sumerian texts
  • Hypothesis of the Anunnaki from Nibiru
  • Genetic engineering of humans by the Anunnaki

Book of Genesis and Book of Enoch ðŸ”—

The Book of Genesis mentions the “sons of God” marrying human women and the Nephilim. The Book of Enoch expands on this, describing the “sons of God” as angels who descended to Earth to breed with humans. Some ancient astronaut proponents argue that this is a historical account of extraterrestrials visiting Earth, called Watchers, who made contact with humans, cross-bred with human females, and shared knowledge with them.

Key Points:

  • Watchers as extraterrestrials who cross-bred with humans
