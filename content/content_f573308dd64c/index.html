This entry focuses on Ashtar, an alleged extraterrestrial being or group of beings, primarily within the context of ufology and UFO religions. It notes the “unbalanced” tag, indicating a need for more diverse viewpoints.

Key points:

  • Ashtar is a name associated with an extraterrestrial being or group of beings.
  • Claims of contact are made through channeling.

Important People:

  • George Van Tassel

Locations:

  • United States

Major Events:

  • First claimed contact in 1952.

History ðŸ”—

This section details the background and development of the Ashtar concept, from its roots in Theosophy to its popularization through UFO contactees.

Key points:

  • Ascended Masters from esoteric teachings are described as reappearing in space suits.
  • The concept of Ascended Masters is clear in the Ashtar teachings.
  • The word “ashtar” appears in Helena Blavatsky’s The Secret Doctrine.

Important People:

  • Helena Blavatsky
  • George Van Tassel

Locations:

  • Giant Rock, California
  • Mojave Desert, California
  • Landers, California

Major Events:

  • Van Tassel moves to Giant Rock in 1947 and establishes a UFO Center.
  • The “Ministry of Universal Wisdom” is founded in 1953.

Purported contact with Van Tassel ðŸ”—

This section focuses on George Van Tassel’s claims of telepathic communication with Ashtar, including the apocalyptic messages he received and his interpretation of the Bible in terms of extraterrestrial intervention.

Key points:

  • Van Tassel claimed to receive messages from Ashtar in 1952.
  • Van Tassel interpreted the Bible in terms of extraterrestrial intervention.
  • Van Tassel claimed to receive instructions from Nikola Tesla to build the “Integratron”.
  • Early messages from Ashtar contained apocalyptic material regarding the hydrogen bomb.
  • Ashtar entered the Solar System as Commander in Chief of the Ashtar Galactic Command to warn humanity of the dangers of detonating the H-bomb.
  • Van Tassel claimed that Ashtar provided specific messages to pass on to the U.S. federal government.
  • Van Tassel claimed to have established a new form of telepathic communication with extraterrestrial intelligences.

Important People:

  • George Van Tassel
  • Nikola Tesla

Major Events:

  • Van Tassel’s first purported contact with Ashtar in 1952.
  • The claimed intervention of Ashtar to prevent Earth’s destruction through nuclear power.

Giant Rock Space Conventions ðŸ”—

This section describes the weekly channeling sessions and annual conventions held at Giant Rock, which helped to coalesce the contactee subculture.

Key points:

  • Van Tassel held weekly channeling sessions at Giant Rock.
  • These gatherings coalesced the scattered contactee subculture into a recognizable movement in January 1952.
  • The annual Giant Rock Spacecraft Convention began in 1953.
  • Most of the well-known UFO contactees attended these conventions as speakers and channelers.
  • Most of the early messages Van Tassel claimed to have received from Ashtar were first presented to the public at these events.

Important People:

  • George Van Tassel
  • J. Gordon Melton

Locations:

  • Giant Rock

Major Events:

  • The first Giant Rock Spacecraft Convention in 1953.

Ashtar Command ðŸ”—

This section details the appropriation of the “Ashtar Command” concept by other contactees and channelers, leading to conflicting messages and a lack of central authority.

Key points:

  • The concept of an “Ashtar Command” was appropriated for use by a number of prominent early contactees and channelers.
  • Robert Short began his own group called “Ashtar Command”.
  • By the mid-1950s, the concept of Ashtar and a galactic law enforcement agency preparing an imminent rescue of humanity had become well-established.
  • The public failure of predictions had a negative impact on the expansion of the Ashtar Command movement.
  • Dozens of Ashtar channelers were presenting conflicting messages.

Important People:

  • George Van Tassel
  • Robert Short
  • Elouise Moeller
  • Adelaide J. Brown

Locations:

  • Giant Rock

Development into spiritual religion (1990s onward) ðŸ”—

This section describes the evolution of the Ashtar message from a technological communication to a more spiritual role, with Ashtar becoming an Ascended Master figure.

Key points:

  • The Ashtar message evolved into a spiritual message.
  • Ashtar began to play more of an Ascended Master role in the narratives.
  • The Ashtar Command evolved into a movement that had a central authority for several decades up to the mid-1990s.
  • The teachings claiming to be channeled from Ashtar varied immensely.
  • Much of the Ashtar channeling is akin to cargo cults.
  • Most of the Ashtar millenarian concepts involve a transformation of human beings, via these technologies, who will then return to the planet Earth to enjoy a golden millennium.
  • With the failure of prophecies, teachings were modified to include a less material and more spiritual emphasis.

Important People:

  • Tuella
  • Andreas Grunschloss

1970s broadcasts ðŸ”—

This section mentions the 1977 Southern Television broadcast interruption, where a voice claiming to be “Vrillon” of the Ashtar Galactic Command took over a television transmitter.

Key points:

  • In 1971, a British radio talk show devoted to UFOs received a strange call-in claiming to originate from outer space.
  • In 1977, a voice calling itself “Vrillon” of the Ashtar Galactic Command temporarily took over a television transmitter in southern England.

Locations:

  • Southern England

Major Events:

  • The 1977 Southern Television broadcast interruption.

1986 prediction of 1994 apocalypse ðŸ”—

This section details Yvonne Cole’s predictions of Earth’s destruction and the arrival of alien cultures in 1994, which ultimately failed to occur.

Key points:

  • Yvonne Cole predicted the destruction of all Earth civilizations and the arrival on the planet of various alien cultures in 1994.
  • Cole claimed that governments were working with extraterrestrials to prepare for contact.
  • The landing would be broadcast through the global media and include a message from the Ashtar Command.
  • These prophecies furthered the continued fracturing and disappointment within the movement when they failed to occur.

Important People:

  • Yvonne Cole

Major Events:

  • The failed 1994 apocalypse prediction.

The 1994 Pioneer Voyage meditations ðŸ”—

This section describes the “lift-off experience” claimed by some Ashtar Command members in 1994, where they were placed aboard “ships of Light” circling the planet.

Key points:

  • A small group of Ashtar Command members claimed that an extraordinary event had taken place: “the lift-off experience”.
  • “The Galactic Fifth Fleet” had used “physical vibrational transfer” which involved the human consciousness (or, sometimes, the “etheric body”) being raised from the physical dimension and transferred to the “Light ships”.
  • A second event was predicted for December 1994 in which over 250 people participated and which was declared by the leadership to have “opened a portal to the AC (Ashtar Command) ships for ever”.
  • The purpose of the lift-off is for the ascension of the human race as a whole, to which individual ascension is a precursor and an aid.
  • The collective ascension is being aided by large electronic grids deployed around the planet by the guardian ships.

Major Events:

  • The 1994 Pioneer Voyage meditations.
