List of Fictional Spacecraft ðŸ”—

This Wikipedia entry catalogs a wide array of fictional spacecraft, starships, and exo-atmospheric vessels that have appeared in various published works of fiction. It distinguishes between “spacecraft,” generally used for vehicles based on present technology, and “spaceship/starship,” terms reserved for fictional vehicles capable of transporting people, often central to science fiction plots.

Space Stations ðŸ”—

This section directs the reader to a separate, more comprehensive list of fictional space stations.

Shuttles ðŸ”—

This section lists fictional shuttles designed for planetary surface-to-orbit travel. Notable examples include:

  • Betty Boop from the Illuminae series, used to prevent timeline collapse.
  • Moonrakers from the James Bond film Moonraker.
  • Orion III (“Pan Am Space Clipper”) from 2001: A Space Odyssey, used to transport Dr. Heywood Floyd.
  • SSTO-TAV-37B Space Shuttlecraft from Avatar.
  • Pathfinder (OV-201) from For All Mankind, a NERVA-powered shuttle capable of cislunar flight.

Space Shuttle Orbiters ðŸ”—

This subsection focuses specifically on fictional Space Shuttle orbiters. Examples include:

  • Churchill from Lifeforce.
  • Daedalus from Space Cowboys.
  • Explorer from Gravity.
  • Excalibur, Henry Henshaw’s shuttle in Superman comics.
  • Intrepid from Payne Harrison’s novel Storming Intrepid.
  • Odyssey from Odyssey 5.
  • Pleiades from David Brin’s novel Earth.
  • Roger, an anthropomorphic Space Shuttle orbiter from Cars Toons.
  • X-71 shuttles (Freedom and Independence) from Armageddon.

Lunar ðŸ”—

This section lists spacecraft designed for lunar missions. Key examples include:

  • Aries Ib, a passenger lunar lander from 2001: A Space Odyssey.
  • Eagle Transporter, Moonbase Alpha’s modular service craft in Space: 1999.
  • Friede, the Moon rocket from the 1929 film Frau im Mond.
  • Mark IX Hawk, a space fighter similar to the Eagle in Space: 1999.
  • Mayflower One from Airplane II: The Sequel.
  • An unnamed “projectile” from Jules Verne’s From the Earth to the Moon.

Interplanetary ðŸ”—

This section details spacecraft capable of traveling between planets. Notable entries include:

  • Aether from The Midnight Sky, returning from a colonization mission to a Jovian moon.
  • Anastasia, Dan Dare’s personal ship.
  • Bebop, the titular ship of Cowboy Bebop.
  • Bilkis, an experimental spaceship in Geneshaft.
  • Cepheus from Ad Astra, used for transport within the Solar System.
  • Democratic Circus, a cruiser from the Revelation Space series.
  • Discovery One, a research spacecraft sent to Jupiter in 2001: A Space Odyssey.
  • Europa One, a research spacecraft from Europa Report sent to Jupiter’s moon Europa.
  • Excelsior from the 1918 film Himmelskibet.
  • F-302 Mongoose, a strike craft in Stargate SG-1.
  • Hermes from The Martian, a spacecraft for the Ares program to Mars.
  • Hunter IV, Samus Aran’s personal fighter from the Metroid series.
  • Icarus I and Icarus II from Sunshine.
  • Lewis & Clark, a rescue ship sent to retrieve the Event Horizon.
  • Mars I and Mars II from Mission to Mars.
  • An unnamed Mars cycler spacecraft from Stowaway.
  • Messiah, an Orion spacecraft from Deep Impact.
  • Nightflyer, the craft from the Nightflyers television series.
  • Odyssey, a spacecraft from Oblivion designed to visit Saturn’s moon Titan.
  • Orbit Jet, from Rocky Jones, Space Ranger.
  • Rocinante, a Martian corvette from The Expanse.
  • Rocket, from Little Einsteins.
  • Ryvius, the title spacecraft from Infinite Ryvius.
  • SA-43 Hammerhead Mk 1, a fighter from Space: Above and Beyond.
  • Scorpio E-X-1, from It’s About Time.
  • Turtle, Cobra’s interplanetary spaceship.
  • SCV-70 White Base, the mothership from Mobile Suit Gundam.
  • USS Cygnus from The Black Hole.
  • Valley Forge, a space freighter from Silent Running.
  • Zero-X from Thunderbirds Are Go.

Interstellar ðŸ”—

This section focuses on spacecraft capable of traveling between stars.

Military or Exploration ðŸ”—

This subsection lists interstellar spacecraft used for military or exploration purposes. Examples include:

  • Access Ark, a planet-sized spacecraft from Kirby: Planet Robobot.
  • Alexander 78v, a battlecarrier from the Illuminae Files.
  • Amaterasu, a Freedom Guard Ship from Starship Operators.
  • Arcadia, a space battleship from Captain Harlock.
  • LCAM-01XA Archangel, a warship from Mobile Suit Gundam SEED.
  • Argonaut, the most advanced starship from Heroic Age.
  • Ark, the Autobots’ flagship from Transformers.
  • Aurora, a starship from Subnautica.
  • Athena, a starship from Solaris.
  • Avalon, a starliner from Passengers.
  • Axalon, a Maximal exploration ship from Beast Wars: Transformers.
  • Axiom, a starliner from WALL-E.
  • Basroil, a starship commanded by Lafiel in Banner of the Stars.
  • Battlestars from Battlestar Galactica.
  • Basestar from Battlestar Galactica.
  • Bellerophon, the ship that took colonists to Altair IV in Forbidden Planet.
  • Blip-A, a ship built by Eridians in Project Hail Mary.
  • C-57D, a cruiser sent to Altair IV in Forbidden Planet.
  • Conquistador, the largest battleship from Starship Operators.
  • Copernicus, an explorer/hauler from the Illuminae Files.
  • Daban Urnud, an interdimensional starship from Anathem.
  • Dark Star.
  • “The Derelict”, an abandoned alien spacecraft from Alien.
  • Darksyde, the Predacon transwarp ship from Beast Wars.
  • Destiny Ascension, an Asari dreadnought from Mass Effect.
  • EAS Agamemnon from Babylon 5.
  • Eltrium, Earth’s flagship from Gunbuster.
  • Endurance, a spacecraft/space station hybrid from Interstellar.
  • Event Horizon, a gravity drive exploration starship.
  • Exelion, a warship from Gunbuster.
  • Hypatia, a scientific exploration vessel from the Illuminae Files.
  • Hyperion, Jim Raynor’s flagship in StarCraft.
  • Hail Mary, a ship sent to Tau Ceti in Project Hail Mary.
  • IMS Draconis, a heavy-transport vessel from Titanfall 2.
  • IMS Malta, a battleship from Titanfall 2.
  • IMS Thermopylae, a transport vessel from Titanfall 2.
  • ISA Excalibur from Babylon 5.
  • Icarus, a near-light speed ship from Planet Of The Apes.
  • Island Cluster Class Colonization Ship 25 from Macross Frontier.
  • JJ-386, a Cobra mkIII piloted by John Jameson in Elite Dangerous.
  • Jupiter 2 from Lost in Space.
  • Justice of Toren, a sentient troop carrier from the Imperial Radch book series.
  • Karrajor, a warship from Megas XLR.
  • Kobayashi Maru, a starship in a Starfleet Academy training simulation in Star Trek.
  • *R.L.S.
