This section introduces the Allianceâ€“Union universe as a future history spanning the third and fourth millennia, focusing on humanity’s expansion into space. History ðŸ”—

This section outlines the chronological development of the Allianceâ€“Union universe. It begins with the establishment of space stations by the Earth Company and the emergence of distinct stationer and merchanter cultures. The discovery of Pell’s World in the Tau Ceti system, with its hospitable environment and alien life (the Hisa), disrupts the existing power balance. The development of faster-than-light (FTL) travel leads to Earth’s attempts to reassert control, culminating in the Company Wars. The Union, centered at the Cyteen system, declares independence and uses genetically engineered clones (azi) in its military. The conflict reaches a turning point at Pell Station, leading to the formation of the Alliance as a neutral power. Conrad Mazian, commander of the Earth Company Fleet, refuses to accept peace and becomes a renegade. Signy Mallory, one of his captains, defects to the Alliance. The Mazianni are eventually driven off, but a new rivalry emerges between the Alliance and Union. The section concludes by mentioning the political factions within Union and the role of scientist Ariane Emory.

This section classifies the various works within the Allianceâ€“Union universe by genre. Many stories set in space are considered space opera, while those on planets are planetary romances. Works like Cyteen and Downbelow Station are also hard science fiction. The “Company Wars” novels and the Faded Sun trilogy are military science fiction. The Merovingen Nights series is classified as science fantasy, and The Morgaine Cycle books are set remotely in time and space. Voyager in Night incorporates elements of horror fiction.
