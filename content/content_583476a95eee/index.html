This Wikipedia article provides a compilation of notable reported sightings of Unidentified Flying Objects (UFOs), including claims of close encounters and alien abduction. It clarifies that UFOs encompass any aerial phenomenon that cannot be immediately identified, noting that most are eventually explained as known objects or atmospheric phenomena. The article also mentions the use of the term “Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena” (UAP) to avoid associations with UFO conspiracy theories.

This section details alleged UFO sightings from ancient times, drawing from historical texts.

  • c. 1450 BC: The Tulli Papyrus describes an event during Thutmose III’s reign in Ancient Egypt, where a “star came down” and set fire to his adversaries.
  • 218 BC: Reports from the Roman Republic in Italia mention “phantom navy” seen shining in the sky.
  • 76 BC: An account from Asia (Roman Republic) describes a “spark” falling from a star, growing to the size of the Moon, and then transforming into a torch.
  • 73 BC: Plutarch recounts a “flame-like body” resembling a wine-jar (pithos) falling between Roman and Pontic armies in Phrygia.
  • AD 65: Flavius Josephus reports chariots “hurtling through the clouds” in Judaea before the First Jewishâ€“Roman War.
  • AD 196: Cassius Dio describes a “fine rain resembling silver” falling from a clear sky in Rome.
