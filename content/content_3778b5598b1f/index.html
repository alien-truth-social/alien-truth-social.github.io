This Wikipedia article provides a compilation of notable reported sightings of Unidentified Flying Objects (UFOs), including claims of close encounters and alien abduction. It clarifies that UFOs encompass any aerial phenomenon that cannot be immediately identified, noting that most are eventually explained as known objects or atmospheric phenomena. The article also mentions the use of the term “Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena” (UAP) to avoid associations with UFO conspiracy theories.

Antiquity ðŸ”—

This section details alleged UFO sightings from ancient times, drawing from historical texts and records.

  • c. 1450 BC: The Tulli Papyrus describes a “star” that came down and set fire to enemies of Thutmose III during a battle in Ancient Egypt.
  • 218 BC: Mentions “ships in the sky” reported over Italia in the Roman Republic.
  • 76 BC: Describes a “spark from a star” that grew to the size of the Moon over Asia in the Roman Republic.
  • 73 BC: Plutarch’s account of a “flame-like body” resembling a wine-jar falling between Roman and Pontic armies in Phrygia.
  • AD 65: Flavius Josephus’s report of chariots “hurtling through the clouds” over Judaea before the First Jewish-Roman War.
  • AD 196: Cassius Dio’s description of a “fine rain resembling silver” falling on the Forum of Augustus in Rome.
