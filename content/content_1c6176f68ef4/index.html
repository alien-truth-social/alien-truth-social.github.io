Premise ðŸ”—

The series follows Philip J. Fry, a pizza delivery boy who is cryogenically preserved for 1,000 years and revived on December 31, 2999. Fry finds work at the interplanetary delivery company Planet Express, working alongside the one-eyed mutant Leela and the robot Bender. Groening envisioned the series in the mid-1990s while working on The Simpsons, and brought David X. Cohen aboard to develop storylines and characters.

Characters ðŸ”—

The plot revolves around the Planet Express interplanetary delivery company and its employees. Episodes usually feature the central trio of Fry, Leela, and Bender, though occasional storylines center on the other main characters.

  • Philip J. Fry: Voiced by Billy West, Fry is an immature, slovenly, yet good-hearted pizza delivery boy who is cryogenically frozen for 1,000 years.
  • Turanga Leela: Voiced by Katey Sagal, Leela is the competent, one-eyed captain of the Planet Express Ship.
  • Bender Bending Rodriguez: Voiced by John DiMaggio, Bender is a foul-mouthed, heavy-drinking, cigar-smoking, kleptomaniacal, misanthropic, egocentric, ill-tempered robot.
  • Professor Hubert J. Farnsworth: Voiced by Billy West, Professor Farnsworth is Fry’s distant nephew and the founder of Planet Express Inc.
  • Hermes Conrad: Voiced by Phil LaMarr, Hermes is the Jamaican accountant of Planet Express.
  • Dr. John A. Zoidberg: Voiced by Billy West, Zoidberg is a Decapodian and the neurotic staff physician of Planet Express.
  • Amy Wong: Voiced by Lauren Tom, Amy is an incredibly rich, blunt, ditzy, and accident-prone intern at Planet Express.

Setting ðŸ”—

Futurama is set in New New York at the turn of the 31st century. The city of New New York has been built over the ruins of present-day New York City, which has become a catacomb-like space that acts as New New York’s sewer, referred to as “Old New York”. Parts of the sewers are inhabited by mutants. Various devices and architecture are similar to the Populuxe style. Global warming, inflexible bureaucracy, and substance abuse are a few of the subjects given a 31st-century exaggeration.

Development ðŸ”—

Fox expressed a strong desire in the mid-1990s for Matt Groening to create a new series after the success of The Simpsons. In 1995, he enlisted David X. Cohen to assist in developing the show. When they pitched the series to Fox in April 1998, Groening and Cohen had composed many characters and story lines. Fox ordered thirteen episodes. Fox feared the themes of the show were not suitable for the network and Groening and Fox executives argued over whether the network would have any creative input into the show. The episode “I, Roommate” was produced to address Fox’s concerns. The name Futurama comes from a pavilion at the 1939 New York World’s Fair.

Writing ðŸ”—

The planning for each episode began with a table meeting of writers, who discussed the plot ideas as a group. A single staff writer wrote an outline and then produced a script. Once the first draft of a script was finished, the writers and executive producers called in the actors for a table read. After this script reading, the writers collaborated to rewrite the script as a group before sending it to the animation team. The writing staff held three Ph.D.s, seven master’s degrees, and cumulatively had more than 50 years at Harvard University.

Voice actors ðŸ”—

Futurama had eight main cast members. Billy West performed the voices of Philip J. Fry, Professor Farnsworth, Doctor Zoidberg, Zapp Brannigan, and many other incidental characters. Katey Sagal voiced Leela, and is the only member of the main cast to voice only one character. Maurice LaMarche voices Kif Kroker and several supporting characters. David Herman voiced Scruffy and various supporting characters.

Animation ðŸ”—

Futurama is produced by The Curiosity Company and 20th Television Animation with the animation being done by Rough Draft Studios. In addition to traditional cartoon drawing, Rough Draft Studios often used CGI for fast or complex shots. The series began high-definition production in season 6, with Bender’s Big Score. For the final episode of season 6, Futurama was completely reanimated in three different styles: Fleischer- and Walter Lantz-style animation, a low-resolution video game, and Japanese anime.

Hallmarks ðŸ”—

Opening sequence ðŸ”—

The Planet Express Ship flies across the screen with the title of the show being spelled out in its wake. Underneath the title is a joke caption. After flying through downtown New New York and past various recurring characters, the Planet Express ship crashes through a large screen showing a short clip from a classic cartoon. The Futurama theme was created by Christopher Tyng.

Languages ðŸ”—

There are three alternative alphabets that appear often in the background of episodes. The first alphabet consists of abstract characters and is referred to as Alienese, a simple substitution cipher from the Latin alphabet. The second alphabet uses a more complex modular addition code. The third language sometimes used is Hebrew. The show predicts that several English expressions will have evolved by the year 3000
