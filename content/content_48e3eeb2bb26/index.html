This is a compilation of songs across various decades that explore lyrical themes related to close encounters with extraterrestrial aliens.

1940s ðŸ”—

This section highlights the earliest example on the list, “(When You See) Those Flying Saucers” by The Buchanan Brothers, released in July 1947. Its release coincided with the beginning of the flying saucer era, shortly after the Kenneth Arnold UFO sighting.

1950s ðŸ”—

This section lists songs from the 1950s that deal with the theme of flying saucers and aliens. Key songs include “The Flying Saucer Parts 1 & 2” by Bill Buchanan and Dickie Goodman, “My Flying Saucer” by Woodie Guthrie, “The Purple People Eater” by Sheb Wooley, “Two Little Men In A Flying Saucer” by Ella Fitzgerald, and “Flyin’ Saucers Rock & Roll” by Billy Lee Riley and Jerry Lee Lewis.

This section includes songs from the 1960s that explore themes of space and extraterrestrial encounters. Notable songs include “Let There Be More Light” and “Set the Controls for the Heart of the Sun” by Pink Floyd, “Mr. Spaceman” by The Byrds, “Voices Green and Purple” by The Bees, “It Came Out of the Sky” by Creedence Clearwater Revival, “Have You Seen the Saucers” by Jefferson Airplane, and “U.F.O.” by Jim Sullivan.
