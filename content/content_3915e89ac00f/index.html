List of alleged UFO sightings in the United States ðŸ”—

This is a compilation of reported UFO sightings within the United States, presented in a table format. Each entry includes the date of the sighting, the city and state where it occurred, a link to a main article if available, and a brief description of the event.

Table of Sightings ðŸ”—

The table lists alleged UFO sightings chronologically, starting from September 1865 and extending to February 2023. Each row provides details about a specific incident.

  • September 1865, Cadotte Pass, Montana: Trapper James Lumley reported seeing a craft and its subsequent crash, noting sulfur smells and hieroglyphics.

  • 1896â€“1897, Multiple Locations, Multiple States: Sightings of airship-like vehicles were reported across the United States during this period, known as the Mystery airship wave.

  • April 17, 1897, Aurora, Texas: Residents claimed an airship crashed, and the deceased alien pilot was buried in the local cemetery.

  • February 24, 1942, Los Angeles, California: The “Battle of Los Angeles” involved an alleged enemy attack and anti-aircraft artillery barrage. Initially thought to be a Japanese attack, it was later attributed to “war nerves” and a possible weather balloon. Secretary of the Navy Frank Knox called the incident a “false alarm.”

  • August 1945, Jornada del Muerto, New Mexico: Two boys reported seeing an “avocado-shaped” object crash, containing insect-like alien creatures, shortly after the first atomic bomb detonation.

  • June 21, 1947, Maury Island, Washington: This incident predates the Kenneth Arnold sighting and involves a reported encounter with “men in black” suits.

  • June 24, 1947, Mount Rainier, Washington: Kenneth Arnold reported seeing nine unusual objects flying in formation near Mount Rainier. His description of the objects’ erratic flight and tremendous speed gained widespread media attention.

  • July 1947, Roswell, New Mexico: The Roswell UFO Incident involves the recovery of materials near Roswell, becoming a subject of intense speculation. The military claims it was a crashed research balloon, while ufologists believe it was an alien craft.

  • January 7, 1948, Maysville, Kentucky: The Mantell Incident involved the death of Captain Thomas F. Mantell, a Kentucky Air National Guard pilot, while pursuing a UFO.

  • July 24, 1948, Montgomery, Alabama: The Chiles-Whitted UFO Encounter involves two commercial pilots reporting a near-collision with a torpedo-shaped object.

  • October 1, 1948, Fargo, North Dakota: The Gorman Dogfight involves a US Air Force pilot sighting and pursuing a UFO for 27 minutes.

  • May 11, 1950, McMinnville, Oregon: Paul and Evelyn Trent took the McMinnville UFO photographs on their farm.

  • May 29, 1950, Mount Vernon, Maryland: Captain Willis Sperry and crewmembers on an American Airlines DC-6 reported a “brilliant, diffused, bluish light.”

  • August 15, 1950, Great Falls, Montana: The Mariana UFO incident garnered national media attention during a period of high interest in UFOs and alien invasion.

  • August 25, 1951, Lubbock, Texas: The Lubbock Lights were a v-shaped formation of lights seen over the town.

  • May 24, 1952, Burbank, California: Orfeo Angelucci claimed to encounter flying saucers and their pilots during his drives home from an aircraft plant.

  • July 13, 1952, Washington, D.C.: The 1952 Washington, D.C. UFO incident involved multiple reports of unidentified flying objects over the city.

  • September 12, 1952, Flatwoods, West Virginia: Six boys and a woman reported seeing a UFO landing and a bizarre-looking creature.

  • November 23, 1953, Sault Ste. Marie, Michigan: Felix Moncla, Jr., a US Air Force pilot, disappeared with 2nd Lt. Robert Wilson while pursuing a UFO over Lake Superior.

  • November 2, 1957, Levelland, Texas: The Levelland UFO case involved multiple witnesses reporting unusual aerial phenomena.

  • September 19, 1961, Lancaster, New Hampshire: Betty and Barney Hill claimed to have been abducted by extraterrestrials, marking the first widely publicized UFO abduction report.

  • April 24, 1964, Socorro, New Mexico: Police officer Lonnie Zamora reported a close encounter with a UFO.

  • September 3, 1965, Exeter, New Hampshire: The Exeter incident involved multiple witnesses, including police officers, observing a large UFO.

  • December 9, 1965, Kecksburg, Pennsylvania: The Kecksburg UFO incident involved a large fireball seen over multiple states and Ontario, Canada, with reports of metal debris.

  • 1969, Leary, Georgia: Jimmy Carter claimed to have seen a UFO and filed a report with the International UFO Bureau.

  • October 11, 1973, Pascagoula, Mississippi: The Pascagoula Abduction involves Charles Hickson and Calvin Parker reporting an alien abduction.

  • January 12, 1975, North Bergen, New Jersey: The Stonehenge incident occurred near the Stonehenge apartment building in North Bergen.

  • November 5, 1975, Snowflake, Arizona: Travis Walton claims to have been abducted by a UFO while working on a logging crew.

  • December 17, 1977, Council Bluffs, Iowa: Local residents reported seeing a reddish fireball and a grass fire where molten metal was recovered.

  • August 27, 1979, Stephen, Minnesota: Deputy Sheriff Val Johnson’s patrol car was struck by a ball of light, causing damage and knocking him unconscious.

  • December 29, 1980, Dayton, Texas: The Cash-Landrum Incident involved a reported UFO sighting and subsequent health issues for the witnesses.

  • November 11, 1987, Gulf Breeze, Florida: The Gulf Breeze UFO incident occurred.

  • March 8, 1994, Multiple Locations, Michigan: Over 100 witnesses reported seeing objects with colored lights across West Michigan.

  • March 13, 1997, Phoenix, Arizona: The Phoenix Lights involved thousands of people witnessing lights of varying descriptions. Former Arizona governor Fife Symington admitted to seeing a delta-shaped craft.

  • January 5, 2000, Highland, Illinois: Five police officers and other witnesses reported a massive, silent, triangular aircraft.

  • August 21, 2004, Chicago, Illinois: A triangular formation of reddish lights was seen by hundreds of witnesses.

  • November 7, 2006, Chicago, Illinois: Airport employees reported a metallic, saucer-shaped craft hovering over O’Hare International Airport.

  • January 5, 2009, Morristown, New Jersey: Five red objects were seen over Morristown. The incident was later revealed to be a hoax by Joe Rudy and Chris Russo.

  • February 19, 2018, Desert, Arizona: Commercial pilots reported seeing an unidentified object, later attributed to a “high-altitude research balloon.”

  • February 21, 2021, Clayton, New Mexico: A pilot reported seeing a long cylindrical object moving rapidly.

  • February 10, 2023, Deadhorse, Alaska: A high-altitude object was shot down over the Beaufort Sea.

  • February 12, 2023, Lake Huron, Michigan: An octagonal object was detected and shot down over Lake Huron.
