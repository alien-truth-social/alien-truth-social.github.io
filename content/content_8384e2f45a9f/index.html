Alienoid (2022 film) ðŸ”—

A 2022 South Korean science fiction action film directed by Choi Dong-hoon, starring Ryu Jun-yeol, Kim Woo-bin, and Kim Tae-ri. The film involves time travel between the Goryeo era and the present day, with the appearance of aliens. It was released in IMAX and 4DX formats. A sequel, Alienoid: Return to the Future, was released in 2024.

Plot ðŸ”—

The plot alternates between the modern day and the Goryeo era.

Prologue: 1380 A.D. ðŸ”—

Monk warriors fail to defeat an alien and its human host. Guard and Thunder, humanoid robots from the future, extract the alien. The dying host asks Guard to care for her baby, but Guard initially refuses due to non-interference policies. Thunder smuggles the infant back with them to the modern day.

Timeline 1: 2012-2022 A.D. ðŸ”—

Guard raises the baby girl, Yi-an, without revealing his true identity. Yi-an becomes suspicious and discovers the truth about Guard, Thunder, and her origins.

Yi-an finds her way to Jisan Hospital, the site of an incoming alien prisoner transport. Guard and Thunder explain alien imprisonment mechanics. A prisoner-aligned alien ship arrives, planning to awaken the Controller and release haava to transform Earth’s atmosphere, awakening all alien prisoners and poisoning humanity. The pilot awakens the Controller and releases haava, devastating the city and awakening other prisoners.

Guard, Thunder, and Yi-an kill the alien pilot and prevent further haava release. Badly wounded, Yi-an travels back in time with the aliens, intending to strand them in the past. Guard is destroyed, Thunder sinks in a river, and the Controller is forced to abandon its host.

Timeline 2: 1391 A.D. ðŸ”—

Ten years after Thunder crash-lands, a Taoist bounty hunter named Mureuk pursues the Divine Blade (Guard’s power source). Mureuk meets martial arts figures like the Monk Guru, the Dual Mages, and Dogturd. He also meets Yi-an (the “Girl Who Shoots Thunder”) and Suiter and Ja-jang, aliens from the future who have taken over a local cult. The Divine Blade and Thunder’s location are key to controlling the haava release.

A chaotic battle ensues. Yi-an lures one alien away, while the other is defeated. Yi-an approaches Thunder’s location.

Monk Guru, the Dual Mages, and Dogturd were all near the initial alien crash and are potential hosts for the Controller. However, Mureuk, Monk Guru’s apprentice, rescued Yi-an and became the Controller’s new host, losing his memories.

Production ðŸ”—

Filming took place over 13 months, with both parts filmed simultaneously. Choi Dong-hoon spent two and a half years writing the script. Jang Young-gyu and Kim Tae-kyung were in charge of music and cinematography, respectively. Yoo Sang-seop and Ryu Seong-cheol served as martial arts directors. Ryu Seong-hee and Lee Ha-jun were the art directors. Dexter Studio handled the CG. Filming locations included Andong, North Gyeongsang Province. The production cost for both parts was estimated at 40 billion won.

Release ðŸ”—

Alienoid was released on July 20, 2022, in South Korea in IMAX and 4DX formats by CJ E&M. Well Go USA acquired North American distribution rights. It was the closing film at the 21st New York Asian Film Festival and screened at the 26th Fantasia International Film Festival. It became available for streaming on Netflix from December 28, 2022.

Reception ðŸ”—

The film received mixed reviews from South Korean critics, with some praising its unique worldview and others criticizing its confusing characters and lore. It was compared to Choi Dong-hoon’s previous film, Jeon Woo-chi: The Taoist Wizard. The film was a box-office failure, requiring 7 million admissions to break even.

Sequel ðŸ”—

Alienoid: Return to the Future was released in January 2024.
