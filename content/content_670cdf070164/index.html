The Dover Demon is a cryptid reportedly sighted in Dover, Massachusetts, in April 1977.

Sightings ðŸ”—

This section details the reported sightings of the Dover Demon in April 1977. William “Bill” Bartlett, John Baxter, and Abby Brabham, all teenagers at the time, independently reported seeing a strange creature. Bartlett described it as a 4-foot tall creature with glowing orange eyes, a watermelon-shaped head, and no nose or mouth, seen on Farm Street. Baxter reported a similar sighting on Miller Hill Road, and Brabham claimed to have seen the creature on Springdale Avenue the following night. The teenagers drew sketches of the creature, with Bartlett swearing to the accuracy of his drawing. The locations of the sightings formed a straight line over 2 miles. The sightings became public in May, drawing comparisons to the Kellyâ€“Hopkinsville encounter. The Mutual UFO Network investigated the sightings, despite no UFOs being reported by the witnesses.

Possible explanations ðŸ”—

This section explores potential explanations for the Dover Demon sightings. One suggestion is that the creature was a foal or a moose calf. Skeptic Joe Nickell proposes that the creature was likely a snowy owl, citing its size, plumage, and the reflection of headlights in its eyes. He suggests that the “long spindly arms” could have been the owl’s partially opened wings. Police suggested the sightings were a school vacation hoax. Skeptic Ben Radford suggests the sightings were influenced by the release of Star Wars and Close Encounters of the Third Kind in 1977.

In popular culture ðŸ”—

This section outlines the Dover Demon’s appearances in popular culture. It was featured in a 2009 episode of the television series Lost Tapes on Animal Planet. The Dover Demon appears as a character in the comic book series Proof and The Perhapanauts, as well as in The Pound: Ghouls Night Out and Hack/Slash: Entry Wound. It also appears in the webcomic Gunnerkrigg Court. Additionally, an alien known as Dover Demon appears in the manga series Dandadan.
