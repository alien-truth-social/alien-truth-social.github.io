The Dover Demon is a creature reportedly sighted in Dover, Massachusetts, in April 1977.

Sightings ðŸ”—

This section details the reported sightings of the Dover Demon in April 1977. William “Bill” Bartlett, John Baxter, and Abby Brabham, all teenagers at the time, independently reported seeing a strange creature. Bartlett described it as about 4 feet tall with glowing orange eyes and no nose or mouth, with a watermelon-shaped head, on Farm Street in Dover. Baxter reported a similar creature on Miller Hill Road, and Brabham claimed to have seen it on Springdale Avenue the following night. The teenagers drew sketches of the creature, with Bartlett swearing on a stack of Bibles to the accuracy of his drawing. The locations of the sightings, when plotted on a map, formed a straight line over 2 miles. The sightings became public in May 1977, and a local investigator noted similarities to the creatures in the Kellyâ€“Hopkinsville encounter. The Mutual UFO Network investigated the sightings, even though no UFOs were reported.

Possible explanations ðŸ”—

This section explores potential explanations for the Dover Demon sightings. One suggestion is that the creature was a foal or a moose calf. Skeptic Joe Nickell proposes it was likely a snowy owl, citing its size, plumage reflecting yellow headlights, and the possibility that the “long spindly arms” were partially opened wings. Police suggested the sightings were a school vacation hoax. Ben Radford suggests the sightings were influenced by the release of Star Wars and Close Encounters of the Third Kind in 1977.

In popular culture ðŸ”—

This section outlines the Dover Demon’s appearances in popular culture. It was featured in an episode of the television series Lost Tapes on Animal Planet in 2009. The Dover Demon appears as a character in the comic book series Proof, where it can see into the future, and in The Perhapanauts. It also made appearances in The Pound: Ghouls Night Out and Hack/Slash: Entry Wound. A Dover Demon appears in the webcomic Gunnerkrigg Court. In the manga series Dandadan, an alien known as Dover Demon takes the form of a crustacean-like alien with the ability to transform into a stronger form.
