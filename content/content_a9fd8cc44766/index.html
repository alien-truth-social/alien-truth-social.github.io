Max Evans (Roswell) ðŸ”—

Maxwell “Max” Evans is a fictional character created by Melinda Metz for the Roswell High book series and adapted for the Roswell television series (1999-2002). He is portrayed by Jason Behr in the original series and by Nathan Parsons in the CW reboot. Max is a central character throughout the series, first appearing in the pilot episode.

Fictional character summary ðŸ”—

This section focuses on the TV character, distinguishing it from the Roswell High book version. Max’s backstory reveals that in a previous life, he was Zan, the king of Antar. After Zan’s death, his DNA and human DNA were used to create alien/human hybrids or clones, including Isabel Evans (Vilandra, Zan’s sister), Michael Guerin (Rath, Vilandra’s betrothed), and Tess Harding (Ava, Zan’s bride). These clones were sent to Earth to escape their enemies, particularly Kivar. The craft carrying Max, Isabel, Michael, and Tess crashed in Roswell, New Mexico, in 1947. The U.S. Army initially took the alien pods, but they were rescued and hidden. The clones emerged in 1989, appearing as children. Max and Isabel were adopted by the Evans, while Michael ended up in foster care.

Characteristics ðŸ”—

Max is presented as shy and introverted, a facade necessitated by his hybrid nature. He is a complex character, displaying both timidity and passion. Despite his initial shyness, he emerges as the leader among his friends. He is portrayed as a deep thinker, often spurred to action by his protective instincts. He takes responsibility for his actions and is known for his unique healing powers.

Powers and abilities ðŸ”—

Max possesses molecular manipulation and telekinesis, abilities shared by the other hybrids. His unique power is healing through physical contact, famously used to save Liz Parker in the pilot episode. He also develops the ability to generate a force field. It is suggested that his healing ability can trigger the development of alien powers in those he heals, such as Liz Parker.

Relationship with Liz Parker ðŸ”—

Max’s love for Liz Parker is a central theme. He has been in love with her since childhood but keeps his distance due to his alien identity. He reveals his secret to save her life after she is shot, initiating the main plot of the show. Their connection is portrayed as deep and mysterious, with Liz able to sense when Max is in danger. Their relationship is considered a core aspect of the show.

Recurring themes ðŸ”—

The relationship between Max and Liz is presented as a modern example of forbidden love, with references to Romeo and Juliet. Maria De Luca refers to them as “modern Romeo and Juliet against the world.” The concept of soulmates is also a recurring theme, emphasizing their deep connection and compatibility.

Jason Behr about his character ðŸ”—

Jason Behr discusses his initial understanding of Max, relating to his search for truth and his place in the world. He highlights the richness of the script and its honest portrayal of relationships and emotions. Behr notes Max’s evolution throughout the show, from being the conscience of the group to learning to allow others to make their own mistakes. He also expresses his open-mindedness about extraterrestrial life.

Reception ðŸ”—

Jason Behr was cast as Max Evans, despite the character’s original description as blond with blue eyes. David Nutter explains that Behr was chosen for his natural talent, humility, calmness, maturity, and wisdom. Melinda Metz, the original books’ writer, approved of the casting choice. Jason Behr won a Young Hollywood Award for his portrayal of Max Evans and received nominations at the Teen Choice Awards and Saturn Awards.
