Alien Hominid ðŸ”—

Alien Hominid is a run and gun video game developed by The Behemoth. It initially launched as a Flash game on Newgrounds in 2002, created by Tom Fulp and Dan Paladin. It was later re-released in expanded versions for various consoles and computers.

Developer and Publisher Information ðŸ”—

The game was developed by The Behemoth, with Dan Paladin as the designer and Tom Fulp as the programmer. Matthew E. Harwood composed the music. The original Flash version was released in 2002. Console versions were released in 2004 and 2005 for PlayStation 2, GameCube, and Xbox. A Game Boy Advance port, developed by Tuna Technologies, came out in 2006. A high-definition version, Alien Hominid HD, was released for Xbox 360 in 2007. A remaster, also titled Alien Hominid HD, was released in 2023 for Windows, Nintendo Switch, Xbox One, and Xbox Series X/S, alongside a sequel, Alien Hominid Invasion. A PlayStation 4 and PlayStation 5 version is planned.

Gameplay ðŸ”—

Alien Hominid is a side-scrolling run and gun shooter, similar to Metal Slug. Gameplay involves instant death upon being hit and supports two-player simultaneous play. Players control the Alien Hominid, who defends against waves of secret agents using a blaster, melee attacks, and grenades. Advanced moves include rolling, biting off heads, digging underground, and collecting power-ups for extra grenades, shields, and unique ammo. Players can also drive vehicles, ride a Yeti, and pilot a UFO. Completing tasks unlocks hats for the Hominid. The main game has sixteen stages. Additional modes include Challenge, Neutron Ball, Pinata Boss, a PDA game with 200 levels and a level editor, All You Can Eat, and the Super Soviet Missile Mastar minigame.

Plot ðŸ”—

The Alien Hominid is flying over Earth when the FBI intercepts its spacecraft, causing it to crash. The FBI steals the ship, leading the alien to pursue them with the help of “fat kids.” After retrieving the ship, the alien crashes into Russia due to a Soviet missile. The alien recovers the ship again but is captured by an Area 51 spaceship. The alien escapes, befriends a sentient eyeball, and defeats a buff alien. The “fat kids” help the alien and eyeball escape in the ship, using a tractor beam to lift the kids out of an Area 51 truck.

Reception ðŸ”—

The game received generally favorable reviews on most platforms, with the Game Boy Advance version receiving universal acclaim. Reviewers praised its old-school gameplay, difficulty, and humor. GameSpot nominated it for “Funniest Game” in 2004.

Legacy ðŸ”—

The Hominid appears in The Behemoth’s Castle Crashers and is a playable character in Super Meat Boy. The Super Soviet Missile Mastar minigame was released as a free iOS app in 2011, and an improved version of the PDA Games minigame was released for iOS in 2011. Tom Fulp cited the PDA Games as inspiration for BattleBlock Theater.

Notes ðŸ”—

Tuna Technologies ported the game to Game Boy Advance and J2ME. Zoo Digital Publishing and O3 Entertainment published the PlayStation 2, GameCube, Xbox, and Game Boy Advance versions in Europe and North America, respectively. Allister Brimble and Anthony N. Putson composed the Game Boy Advance music.
