Alien Hominid ðŸ”—

Alien Hominid is a run and gun video game developed by The Behemoth. It initially launched as a Flash game on Newgrounds in 2002, created by Tom Fulp and Dan Paladin. Later, expanded versions were released on various consoles and computers.

Gameplay ðŸ”—

This section details the gameplay mechanics of Alien Hominid, comparing it to games like Metal Slug. The game is a side-scrolling run and gun shooter where one hit kills the player. Players control the titular alien, fending off waves of secret agents using a blaster, melee attacks, and grenades. Advanced moves include rolling, head-biting, and digging underground. Power-ups provide extra grenades, shields, and unique ammo. Players can also drive vehicles, ride a Yeti, and pilot a UFO. Completing tasks unlocks hats for the Hominid. The main game consists of sixteen stages. Additional multiplayer modes include Challenge, Neutron Ball, and Pinata Boss. There’s also a PDA game with around 200 levels and a level editor, an extra mode called All You Can Eat, and a retro minigame, Super Soviet Missile Mastar.

Plot ðŸ”—

This section outlines the game’s storyline. The Alien Hominid’s spacecraft is intercepted by the FBI, causing it to crash on Earth. The FBI steals the ship, leading the alien to pursue them with the help of “fat kids.” After retrieving the ship, the alien crashes into a Soviet missile, landing in Russia. The alien recovers the ship again, only to be captured by an Area 51 spaceship. The alien escapes, befriends a sentient eyeball, and defeats a buff alien. The “fat kids” help the alien and eyeball escape in the alien’s ship, using a tractor beam to lift the kids out of an Area 51 truck.

Reception ðŸ”—

This section summarizes the critical reception of Alien Hominid across different platforms. The game received generally favorable reviews, with high scores from publications like GameRankings, Metacritic, Edge, Electronic Gaming Monthly, Eurogamer, Game Informer, GamePro, Game Revolution, GameSpot, GameSpy, GameTrailers, GameZone, IGN, Nintendo Power, Official U.S. PlayStation Magazine, Official Xbox Magazine, Detroit Free Press, and The Times. The game was praised for its old-school gameplay, difficulty, and humor. It won awards at the Independent Games Festival.

Legacy ðŸ”—

This section discusses the game’s impact and references in other games. The Hominid appears in The Behemoth’s Castle Crashers and is playable in Super Meat Boy. The Super Soviet Missile Mastar minigame was released as a free iOS app. The PDA Games minigame inspired The Behemoth’s BattleBlock Theater. In 2020, The Behemoth announced Alien Hominid Invasion, a reimagining of the original game, released in 2023 alongside a remaster of the original, Alien Hominid HD.

Notes ðŸ”—

This section provides additional information about the game’s development and publishing. Tuna Technologies ported the game to Game Boy Advance and J2ME. Zoo Digital Publishing and O3 Entertainment published the PlayStation 2, GameCube, Xbox, and Game Boy Advance versions in Europe and North America. Allister Brimble and Anthony N. Putson composed the Game Boy Advance music.
