Alien üîó

The Alien franchise is a science fiction horror and action media franchise that began with the 1979 film Alien. The series centers on Ellen Ripley’s battles with the extraterrestrial Xenomorph and the prequel series following the android David 8 and the Engineers. The franchise is owned by 20th Century Studios.

Created by: Dan O’Bannon and Ronald Shusett Original Work: Alien (1979) Owner: 20th Century Studios Years Active: 1979‚Äìpresent

Premise üîó

The franchise depicts deadly encounters between humanity and the Xenomorph, a hostile extraterrestrial species. Humanity is a space-faring species, and space journeys require cryosleep. The megacorporation Weyland-Yutani Corp seeks to profit from the Aliens, manipulating and endangering characters. The series also explores the origin of the human race, with the Engineers sacrificing themselves to spark the genesis of mankind.

Background üîó

Dan O’Bannon and Ronald Shusett collaborated on the script for Alien. Brandywine Productions bought the script, and 20th Century Fox invested millions after the success of Star Wars. The Ripley character was initially to be played by Tom Skerritt, but Sigourney Weaver was cast instead. Ridley Scott directed the film. H. R. Giger designed the alien creature and derelict ship, M≈ìbius created the spacesuits, and Ron Cobb provided the industrial design for the sets.

James Cameron expressed interest in continuing the Alien story in 1983. After Cameron’s The Terminator became a hit, he and Gale Anne Hurd were approved to direct and produce Aliens. Following the second film, Weaver was not interested in returning, but she was offered a $5 million salary and a producer credit to make Alien 3. Alien 3 faced script difficulties, trouble securing a director, and studio interference.

Production on Alien Resurrection began in 1996. Weaver joined the project after being offered an $11 million salary and more creative control. The script resurrected the Ripley character via human cloning. Joss Whedon later said that he thought it had done “everything wrong” with his script.

This section lists the Alien films, including the original series, Alien vs. Predator films, and Prometheus films. It includes the U.S. release date, director, screenwriter(s), story by, and producer(s) for each film.

Original series üîó

  • Alien (1979): Directed by Ridley Scott, starring Sigourney Weaver as Ellen Ripley. The crew of the Nostromo encounters a deadly alien creature.
  • Aliens (1986): Directed by James Cameron, Ripley returns to LV-426 with a squad of marines to investigate a lost colony.
  • Alien 3 (1992): Directed by David Fincher, Ripley crash-lands on a prison planet and faces a new alien threat.
  • Alien Resurrection (1997): Directed by Jean-Pierre Jeunet, Ripley is cloned 200 years after her death, and the military attempts to exploit the Aliens.

Crossover series üîó

  • Alien vs. Predator (2004): Directed by Paul W. S. Anderson, a group of explorers discovers an ancient pyramid in Antarctica, where they become caught in a battle between Aliens and Predators.
  • Aliens vs. Predator: Requiem (2007): Directed by Greg & Colin Strause, the Predalien hybrid from the previous film causes a Predator ship to crash in Gunnison, Colorado, leading to a battle between Aliens and a Predator.

Prequel series üîó

  • Prometheus (2012): Directed by Ridley Scott, scientists Elizabeth Shaw and Charlie Holloway discover a star map and travel to LV-223, where they encounter the Engineers.
  • Alien: Covenant (2017): Directed by Ridley Scott, the crew of the Covenant traces a transmission to an Engineer homeworld, where they encounter David 8 and a new breed of Alien.
