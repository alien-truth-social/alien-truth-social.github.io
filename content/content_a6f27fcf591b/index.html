This is a compilation of songs across various decades that explore lyrical themes related to close encounters with extraterrestrial aliens.

1940s ðŸ”—

This section highlights the earliest example on the list, “(When You See) Those Flying Saucers” by The Buchanan Brothers, released in July 1947. Its release coincided with the beginning of the flying saucer era, shortly after the Kenneth Arnold UFO sighting.

1950s ðŸ”—

The 1950s saw a surge in songs about flying saucers and aliens. Notable entries include “The Flying Saucer Parts 1 & 2” by Bill Buchanan and Dickie Goodman, “My Flying Saucer” with lyrics by Woodie Guthrie, “The Purple People Eater” by Sheb Wooley, “Two Little Men In A Flying Saucer” by Ella Fitzgerald, and “Flyin’ Saucers Rock & Roll” by Billy Lee Riley and Jerry Lee Lewis.

1960s ðŸ”—

This section lists songs from the 1960s that touch on extraterrestrial themes. Key songs include “Let There Be More Light” and “Set the Controls for the Heart of the Sun” by Pink Floyd, “Mr. Spaceman” by The Byrds, “Voices Green and Purple” by The Bees, “It Came Out of the Sky” by Creedence Clearwater Revival, “Have You Seen the Saucers” by Jefferson Airplane, and “U.F.O.” by Jim Sullivan.

The 1970s brought a diverse range of songs exploring alien encounters. Highlights include “After the Gold Rush” by Neil Young, “Calling Occupants of Interplanetary Craft” by Klaatu (covered by The Carpenters), “Childhood’s End” by Pink Floyd, “Come Sail Away” by Styx, “Here Come the Martian Martians” by Jonathan Richman and the Modern Lovers, “Horsell Commons and the Heat Ray” by Jeff Wayne, “I’ve Seen the Saucers” by Elton John, “Longer Boats” by Cat Stevens, “Martian Boogie” by Brownsville Station, “Mothership Connection” by Parliament, “Starman” by David Bowie, “Starship Trooper” and “Arriving UFO” by Yes, “Waiting for the UFOs” by Graham Parker, “Silver Lights” by Sammy Hagar, “UFO” by Johnny Rivers, “Children of the Sun” by Billy Thorpe, “Starrider” by Foreigner, “Strange Ships” by Fox, and “Ships in the Sky” by Wishbone Ash.
