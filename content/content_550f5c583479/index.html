# Solway Firth Spaceman

This article discusses the "Solway Firth Spaceman," a figure appearing in a photograph taken in 1964 by Jim Templeton. The photograph has become a subject of interest for ufologists and paranormal investigators.

## Photograph

This section describes the circumstances surrounding the photograph.

*   **Date and Location:** The photo was taken on May 23, 1964, on Burgh Marsh, near Burgh by Sands, overlooking the Solway Firth in Cumberland, England.
*   **Photographer:** Jim Templeton, a firefighter, photographer, and local historian.
*   **Subject:** Templeton was photographing his five-year-old daughter.
*   **Claim:** Templeton claimed that the photograph shows a figure in the background resembling a spaceman, but he insists he didn't see anyone there when he took the picture.
*   **Confirmation:** Kodak analysts confirmed the photograph was genuine.

## Explanation of the "spaceman"

This section presents the most widely accepted explanation for the figure in the photograph.

*   **David Clarke's Explanation:** Journalist David Clarke suggests the "spaceman" is likely Templeton's wife, Annie.
*   **Reasoning:** Clarke argues that Annie was present, wearing a pale blue dress, and the overexposure of the photograph made the dress appear white. The camera's viewfinder only showed 70% of the shot, so Templeton may not have seen her.
*   **Image Manipulation:** Darkening the image and straightening the horizon using photo software makes the figure appear more like a regular person viewed from behind.

## Publicity

This section details the aftermath and publicity surrounding the photograph.

*   **Initial Reaction:** Templeton took the photo to the police, who found nothing suspicious. The local newspaper picked up the story, and it spread worldwide.
*   **Templeton's Stance:** Templeton maintained that the photo was not a fake and that he was as puzzled as anyone about the figure's appearance.
*   **"Men in Black" Encounter:** Templeton claimed he was visited by two men who identified themselves only by number and said they worked for the government. They became angry when he said he didn't see the figure at the time and left him to walk home. He later dismissed them as frauds.
*   **Blue Streak Missile Claim:** Templeton also claimed that a Blue Streak missile launch in Australia was aborted because figures resembling the "spaceman" were seen on the firing range.
*   **Ministry of Defence Response:** The British Ministry of Defence stated that the Templeton photo was of no interest to them.
*   **Daughter's Perspective:** In a 1996 interview, Templeton's daughter, Elizabeth Dobson, commented on the attention the photo received and speculated that the figure could be from another planet.
*   **Later Life:** Shortly before his death in 2011, Templeton mentioned that his daughter and grandson were taking over as custodians of his historical library.
