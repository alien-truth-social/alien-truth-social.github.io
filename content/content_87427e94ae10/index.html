This Wikipedia page serves as a dynamic list of notable reported sightings of Unidentified Flying Objects (UFOs), also referred to as Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena (UAP). It includes instances with claims of close encounters and alien abduction. The list acknowledges that most UFOs are eventually identified as known objects or phenomena, but a small percentage remain unexplained.

Antiquity ðŸ”—

This section details alleged UFO sightings from ancient times, drawing from historical texts and records.

  • c. 1450 BC: Tulli Papyrus: Describes an event during Thutmose III’s reign in Ancient Egypt, where a “star came down” and set fire to his adversaries. The account is based on the Tulli Papyrus.
  • 218 BC: Ships in the sky: A “phantom navy” was seen shining in the sky over Italia during the Roman Republic.
  • 76 BC: Spark from a falling star: A spark fell from a star in Asia (Roman province), grew to the size of the Moon, ascended, and transformed into a torch.
  • 73 BC: Flame-like wine-jars from the sky: During a battle between Lucullus and Mithridates VI of Pontus in Phrygia, a “huge, flame-like body” shaped like a wine-jar (pithos) fell between the two armies.
  • AD 65: Sky army: Flavius Josephus reported chariots “hurtling through the clouds” over Judaea before the First Jewishâ€“Roman War.
  • AD 196: Angel hair: Cassius Dio described a “fine rain resembling silver” descending from a clear sky upon the Forum of Augustus in Rome.
