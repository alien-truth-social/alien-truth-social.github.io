James Albert Harder was a professor of civil and hydraulic engineering at the University of California, Berkeley, and later a professor emeritus. He also had a notable interest in ufology.

Engineering ðŸ”—

Harder taught civil engineering at various levels, emphasizing practical aspects like lab experiments and model testing. While he didn’t publish extensively, his work was recognized for its quality. He made significant contributions to flow simulations before computers were widely used. A paper he co-authored, Sea Water Intrusion in California, received an award in 1957. His work was applied in Suisun Bay and the Mekong Delta, and he also studied fish protection facilities. Later in his career, he studied fluid mechanics in a medical context, working on artificial hearts, endoscopes, and colostomy equipment.

Ufology ðŸ”—

Harder believed that unidentified flying objects were extraterrestrial beings. He testified before the House of Representatives Committee on Science and Astronautics and was involved with the Aerial Phenomena Research Organization (APRO).
