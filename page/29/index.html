Culture of Extraterrestriality ðŸ”— The culture of extraterrestriality encompasses the cultural imagination and depiction of otherworldliness, alienness, or outer space. It characterizes “the other” through extraterrestrial space, going beyond mere extraterritoriality or periphery. It involves the space imagined or described as extraterrestrial, or any space outside a described land, creating conditions of spatially set apart otherness. Extraterrestriality has been a feature of many past and contemporary cultures. Politically, it has been an element of utopianism and colonialism, particularly in the formation of colonies as products in extraterrestrial space.
