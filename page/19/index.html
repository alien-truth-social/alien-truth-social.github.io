Flying Saucer ðŸ”— A flying saucer, or flying disc, is a purported type of disc-shaped unidentified flying object (UFO). The term originated in 1947 when US news media reported pilot Kenneth Arnold’s claim of seeing objects flying near his airplane over Washington State. The news reports included implausible speed estimates and preceded a wave of sightings across the US, including the Roswell incident and the Flight 105 UFO sighting. A National Guard pilot died in 1948 while pursuing a flying saucer.

Mariana UFO film

Tags: UFO Sighting Film Evidence
Mariana UFO Incident ðŸ”— The Mariana UFO incident occurred in August 1950 in Great Falls, Montana. It involves a sighting filmed by local baseball coach Nick Mariana, considered among the first motion picture footage of a UFO. The U.S. Air Force initially claimed the objects were reflections of two F-94 jet fighters, a claim they later retracted and then endorsed again. Multiple investigations have yielded inconclusive results. Incident ðŸ”— On August 15, 1950, at 11:29 am, Nick Mariana and his secretary, Virginia Raunig, spotted two bright, silvery objects rotating while flying over Great Falls at an estimated speed of 200 to 400 miles per hour.

Time-traveler UFO hypothesis ðŸ”— The time-traveler hypothesis, also known as chrononaut UFO, future humans, extratempestrial model, and Terminator theory, proposes that unidentified flying objects (UFOs) are humans traveling from the future using advanced technology. This concept has gained recent attention from figures like retired NASA aerospace engineer Larry Lemke, Wisconsin congressman Mike Gallagher, and filmmaker Steven Spielberg. Mainstream scholars consider this hypothesis extremely implausible, and it’s generally viewed as unorthodox even among fringe conspiracy theorists.

UFO flap

2025/03/12
Categories: Ufology
Tags: UFO Flaps Sightings
UFO Flap ðŸ”— A UFO flap refers to a sudden increase in reports of unidentified flying objects (UFOs). The term “flap,” originally a military term for a period of panic or chaos, is used by ufologists to describe surges of sightings in a specific geographical area. Larger surges spanning multiple countries are sometimes called “waves.” The frequency of UFO sightings, public interest, and news coverage varies significantly. The terms UFO, flap, and wave allow for the discussion of various unrelated phenomena in the night sky as if they are part of the same aerial event.
