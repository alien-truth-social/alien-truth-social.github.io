Tags: Mars Lander Water_Ice
Phoenix (NASA Mars Lander) The Phoenix was a NASA uncrewed space probe that landed on Mars on May 25, 2008, and operated until November 2, 2008. It was designed to assess the local habitability and research the history of water on Mars. The mission was part of the Mars Scout Program and cost $420 million. Mission Overview The mission had two primary goals: to study the geological history of water on Mars and to evaluate past or potential planetary habitability in the ice-soil boundary.

Kepler The Kepler space telescope, a NASA mission from 2009 to 2018, was designed to discover Earth-sized exoplanets. Named after astronomer Johannes Kepler, it operated in a heliocentric orbit. The mission ended when its reaction control system fuel was depleted. Mission type The Kepler mission was a space telescope mission. UFO Legend Caused by 1947 Balloon Crash: Roswell Incident The Roswell incident is a cornerstone of UFO lore, stemming from the recovery of debris near Roswell, New Mexico, in 1947. Initially reported as a “flying disc” capture, the U.S. Army quickly retracted the statement, claiming it was a weather balloon. This event, coupled with subsequent government explanations and retractions, fueled decades of conspiracy theories alleging a cover-up of extraterrestrial contact.
