An unidentified flying object (UFO), also referred to as an unidentified anomalous phenomenon (UAP), is any aerial object or phenomenon that cannot be immediately identified or explained. While historical reports of unusual aerial sightings date back to ancient times, the term gained cultural prominence following World War II, leading to various government investigations, such as the U.S. Air Force’s Project Blue Book. Although scientific and skeptical organizations, including the Committee for Skeptical Inquiry, consistently find that the majority of these reports are misidentifications of natural phenomena, human technology, or hoaxes, a small percentage remain unexplained. In recent years, entities such as NASA’s UAP independent study team and the Department of Defense’s All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office have continued to analyze data regarding these encounters.
