Cryptoterrestrial hypothesis

The cryptoterrestrial hypothesis suggests that unidentified aerial phenomena (UFOs) are evidence of an advanced, indigenous civilization hidden on Earth rather than extraterrestrial visitors.

Apr 30, 2026 1 minute read

The cryptoterrestrial hypothesis suggests that unidentified aerial phenomena (UFOs) are evidence of an advanced, indigenous civilization hidden on Earth rather than extraterrestrial visitors. The concept draws from late 19th-century literature and mid-20th-century fringe theories, such as the “Shaver Mystery,” to propose that these beings operate from subterranean or hidden environments. While scholars often frame the hypothesis as a thought experiment, the idea remains a recurring theme in modern ufology, appearing in recent discussions by figures like Luis Elizondo. Additionally, the theme of hidden Earth-based civilizations is frequently explored in popular media, including films like The Abyss and Nope, which utilize these narratives to create speculative science-fiction scenarios.
