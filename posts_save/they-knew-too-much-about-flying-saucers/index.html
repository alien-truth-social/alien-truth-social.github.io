They Knew Too Much About Flying Saucers

*They Knew Too Much About Flying Saucers* is a 1956 book by Gray Barker that holds historical significance as the first text to introduce the "Men in Black" trope into UFO conspiracy culture.

Apr 30, 2026 1 minute read

They Knew Too Much About Flying Saucers is a 1956 book by Gray Barker that holds historical significance as the first text to introduce the “Men in Black” trope into UFO conspiracy culture. Barker, a member of the International Flying Saucer Bureau, alleged that researchers like Albert K. Bender were being intimidated and silenced by mysterious figures to prevent the disclosure of extraterrestrial truths. While the book became a bestseller and influenced decades of paranormal folklore, later investigations—including comments by collaborator John Sherwood—suggest that Barker significantly embellished or fabricated these accounts to capitalize on public interest. The work ultimately shaped the foundational imagery for modern pop culture franchises, including The X-Files and the Men in Black film series.
