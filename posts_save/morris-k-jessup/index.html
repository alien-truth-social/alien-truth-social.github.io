Morris K. Jessup

Morris K.

Apr 30, 2026 1 minute read

Morris K. Jessup (1900–1959) was an American astronomer and ufologist who gained notoriety for his 1955 book, The Case for the UFO. In his writing, Jessup proposed that unidentified flying objects were extraterrestrial in origin and linked them to ancient archaeological sites and prehistoric civilizations. He later became a central figure in the legend of the “Philadelphia Experiment” after receiving correspondence from Carl Meredith Allen, which detailed claims of a secret U.S. Navy naval invisibility project. Jessup died by suicide in Florida on April 20, 1959, following a period of personal instability and declining professional success.
