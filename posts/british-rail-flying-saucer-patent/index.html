British Rail flying saucer patent

The British Rail flying saucer, officially termed "space vehicle," was a proposed interplanetary spacecraft designed by Charles Osmond Frederick.

Jul 21, 2025 1 minute read

The British Rail flying saucer, officially termed “space vehicle,” was a proposed interplanetary spacecraft designed by Charles Osmond Frederick. A patent application was filed for this craft by British Rail in December 1970 and granted in March 1973. The design, which evolved from a lifting platform to a large passenger craft, was to be powered by controlled thermonuclear fusion using laser beams. This concept was later examined by nuclear scientists who deemed it unworkable and inefficient, noting that many of the required technologies had not yet been discovered.
