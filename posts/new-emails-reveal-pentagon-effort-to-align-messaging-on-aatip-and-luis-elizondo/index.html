New Emails Reveal Pentagon Effort to Align Messaging on AATIP and Luis Elizondo

Newly released Pentagon emails from May 2019 reveal an internal contradiction regarding Luis Elizondo's role in the Advanced Aerospace Threat Identification Program (AATIP).

Apr 27, 2026 1 minute read

Newly released Pentagon emails from May 2019 reveal an internal contradiction regarding Luis Elizondo’s role in the Advanced Aerospace Threat Identification Program (AATIP). A senior Pentagon official, Neill Tipton, stated in an email that Elizondo had no assigned responsibilities related to AATIP during his time under Tipton. However, other emails show Tipton was directly communicating with Elizondo about AATIP and reviewing a memo concerning responsibilities for it. Pentagon officials expressed concern about these contradictions and the need for consistent messaging. Despite Tipton’s 2019 statement, previously released records show him engaging in direct discussions with Elizondo about AATIP responsibilities in 2017. Luis Elizondo disputes Tipton’s characterization, stating that documentary evidence shows Tipton was aware of his involvement and agreed to assume his responsibilities.
